## Phase 1: Strategic Foundation

### Campaign Objectives
**Define SMART Goals:**
- **Specific**: Exactly what do you want to achieve?
- **Measurable**: How will you track success?
- **Achievable**: Is this realistic given resources?
- **Relevant**: Does this align with business objectives?
- **Time-bound**: What's the timeline?

**Common Campaign Types:**
- Brand awareness
- Product launch
- Lead generation
- Community building
- Crisis response
- Seasonal/event-driven

### Target Audience Definition
Use the AU/Western 20-35 framework:

**Primary Audience:**
- Demographics (age, location, income)
- Psychographics (values, interests, lifestyle)
- Digital behaviour (platforms, content preferences)
- Pain points and motivations

**Secondary Audience:**
- Influencers in the space
- Media and journalists
- Industry peers
- Existing customers who can advocate

### Competitive Landscape Analysis
- What are competitors doing in this space?
- What messages are they using?
- Which channels are saturated vs. underutilized?
- What white space opportunities exist?

## Phase 2: Message Strategy

### Key Message Development
**Single Sentence Summary:**
"We help [target audience] achieve [desired outcome] through [unique approach] because [proof/reason to believe]."

**Supporting Messages (3-5 max):**
- Functional benefit
- Emotional benefit
- Proof point/credibility
- Community/belonging benefit

### Tone & Voice for Campaign
**Campaign-Specific Tone:**
- Build on established brand voice
- Adapt for campaign context
- Consider current cultural moment
- Align with audience mindset

**Language Register for AU/Western 20-35:**
- Conversational but not try-hard
- Self-aware, embraces irony
- Direct without aggression
- Emoji-literate (use sparingly, purposefully)
- Avoids corporate jargon, buzzwords, hustle culture

## Phase 3: Channel Strategy & Content Planning

### Platform Selection
**Considerations for each platform:**
- Where is your audience most active?
- What content formats work best?
- Organic vs. paid opportunities
- Creator partnership potential

**Platform-Specific Strategies:**
- **Instagram**: Visual storytelling, Stories, Reels
- **TikTok**: Authentic, trend-driven content
- **LinkedIn**: Professional, thought leadership
- **Twitter/X**: Real-time engagement, conversations
- **Email**: Owned audience, deeper relationships

### Creator Partnership Strategy
**Creator Tiers & Objectives:**
- **Nano (1K-10K)**: Authenticity, community building, testing
- **Micro (10K-100K)**: Product launches, UGC generation, sustained partnerships
- **Mid-Tier (100K-500K)**: Awareness campaigns, brand positioning
- **Macro (500K-1M)**: Major launches, repositioning
- **Mega (1M+)**: Cultural moments, brand prestige

**Creator Brief Elements:**
- Campaign objectives and key messages
- Content requirements and deliverables
- Brand guidelines and do's/don'ts
- Timeline and approval process
- Compensation and usage rights

### Content Calendar Development
**Content Types:**
- Hero content (big moments, announcements)
- Hub content (regular, valuable content)
- Hygiene content (FAQs, product info)

**Publishing Schedule:**
- Pre-launch teasers
- Launch moment amplification
- Sustained engagement content
- Post-campaign analysis content

## Phase 4: Creative Direction & Asset Development

### Visual Identity for Campaign
**Consistent with brand but campaign-specific:**
- Color palette adaptation
- Typography selections
- Photography/illustration style
- Video/motion treatment
- Branded hashtag or campaign mark

### Copy Development
**Headlines & CTAs:**
- Lead with benefit, not feature
- Use active voice
- Create urgency without pressure
- Test multiple variations

**Long-form Copy:**
- Problem/solution narrative structure
- Include social proof and testimonials
- Address objections proactively
- End with clear next step

### Asset Specifications
**Required Deliverables:**
- Social media post templates
- Story templates
- Video/animation specs
- Email templates
- Landing page design
- Creator partnership assets
- PR/media kit materials

## Phase 5: Launch & Optimization

### Campaign Timeline
**Pre-Launch (2-4 weeks):**
- Asset creation and approval
- Creator outreach and briefing
- Audience seeding and anticipation
- Media relations
- Internal team alignment

**Launch Week:**
- Hero moment execution
- Real-time social listening
- Creator content amplification
- Paid media activation
- PR push

**Post-Launch (2-8 weeks):**
- Sustained content publishing
- Community engagement
- Performance optimization
- UGC collection and amplification
- Results analysis

### Performance Monitoring
**Key Metrics by Objective:**
- **Awareness**: Reach, impressions, brand mention volume
- **Engagement**: Comments, shares, saves, time spent
- **Conversion**: Clicks, sign-ups, purchases, downloads
- **Community**: Follower growth, UGC creation, advocate development

**Weekly Check-ins:**
- What's working better than expected?
- What's underperforming and why?
- Are there unexpected opportunities?
- What optimizations can be made immediately?

## Phase 6: Analysis & Learning

### Campaign Performance Report
**Executive Summary:**
- Did we achieve our objectives?
- What was the ROI/ROAS?
- Key learnings and insights

**Detailed Analysis:**
- Performance by channel
- Content that resonated most/least
- Audience insights discovered
- Creator partnership results
- Competitive response

### Optimization Recommendations
**For Future Campaigns:**
- Which strategies should be repeated?
- What channels deserve more investment?
- Which creators were most effective?
- What content formats drove best results?
- How can we improve efficiency?

### Knowledge Capture
- Update audience personas with new insights
- Refine messaging based on resonance
- Build on successful creative approaches
- Document learnings for team knowledge base
</process>

<success_criteria>
Campaign strategy is complete when:
- Clear, measurable objectives are defined
- Target audience is precisely identified and understood
- Key messages resonate with audience values and language
- Channel strategy maximizes reach and engagement potential
- Creative direction is distinctive and on-brand
- Timeline and deliverables are realistic and actionable
- Success metrics and optimization framework are established
- Campaign brief is comprehensive enough for team execution
- LGBTQ+ inclusive and community-first principles are embedded throughout
</success_criteria>
