# Colour Theory for Brand & Marketing

## Colour Psychology Foundations

### Primary Colour Associations (Western Context)

**Red**
- Energy, urgency, passion, appetite
- Use: CTAs, sale messaging, food/beverage
- Caution: Can signal danger/error

**Blue**
- Trust, calm, professionalism, reliability
- Use: Finance, tech, healthcare
- Caution: Can feel cold, corporate

**Yellow**
- Optimism, warmth, attention, caution
- Use: Highlights, youth brands, hospitality
- Caution: Hard to read, can cause fatigue

**Green**
- Growth, nature, health, money
- Use: Sustainability, wellness, finance
- Caution: Overused in eco-washing

**Purple**
- Creativity, luxury, spirituality, mystery
- Use: Premium brands, creative industries
- Caution: Can feel artificial

**Orange**
- Enthusiasm, creativity, affordable energy
- Use: Entertainment, food, youth markets
- Caution: Can cheapen premium positioning

**Pink**
- Playfulness, romance, warmth, challenge
- Use: Beauty, inclusive brands, challenger positioning
- Caution: Gendered assumptions

**Black**
- Sophistication, power, elegance, edge
- Use: Luxury, fashion, premium tech
- Caution: Can feel oppressive in volume

**White**
- Purity, simplicity, space, modernity
- Use: Minimalist brands, healthcare, tech
- Caution: Can feel sterile, empty

## Distinctive Colourway Strategies

### 1. Unexpected Pairings
Combine colours that create tension:
- Mustard + Lavender
- Coral + Forest Green
- Electric Blue + Burnt Orange
- Sage + Hot Pink

### 2. Duotone Systems
Two-colour systems with maximum impact:
- High contrast: Creates energy
- Analogous: Creates harmony
- Complementary: Creates vibration

### 3. Muted Meets Saturated
Combine desaturated bases with saturated accents:
- Grey-blue base + Electric coral accent
- Sage green base + Cobalt accent
- Warm taupe base + Bright yellow accent

### 4. Gradient Systems
Movement and depth through gradients:
- Sunset gradients (warm-to-warm shifts)
- Ocean gradients (cool temperature variations)
- Unexpected gradients (unrelated hue shifts)

### 5. Limited Palette Mastery
Constraint breeds creativity:
- 2-colour systems force bold choices
- 3-colour maximum for most brands
- Tint/shade variations within palette

## Accessibility Requirements

### WCAG Contrast Ratios

**Text on Background:**
- Normal text: 4.5:1 minimum
- Large text (18px+ or 14px bold): 3:1 minimum
- Logo/decorative: No requirement

**Tools:**
- WebAIM Contrast Checker
- Stark plugin (Figma/Sketch)
- Coolors accessibility checker

### Colour Blindness Considerations

**Types to design for:**
- Protanopia (red-blind)
- Deuteranopia (green-blind)
- Tritanopia (blue-blind)

**Design strategies:**
- Never rely on colour alone for meaning
- Use icons, labels, patterns alongside colour
- Test with colour blindness simulators
- Avoid red/green only distinctions

## Cultural Colour Considerations

### Research Required for International Markets

**White:**
- Western: Purity, weddings
- Some Asian cultures: Mourning, death

**Red:**
- Western: Danger, passion
- Chinese: Luck, prosperity, celebration

**Yellow:**
- Western: Caution, happiness
- Some cultures: Sacred, imperial

**Green:**
- Western: Nature, go signal
- Some Middle Eastern: Sacred colour

**Always research target market colour associations before finalising palette.**

## Colour System Structure

### Primary Palette (60%)
- 2-3 colours used most frequently
- Forms brand recognition foundation
- Used in: Logo, major UI elements, hero content

### Secondary Palette (30%)
- 2-3 supporting colours
- Extends visual range
- Used in: Backgrounds, secondary elements, categories

### Accent Palette (10%)
- 1-2 high-impact colours
- Used sparingly for maximum effect
- Used in: CTAs, highlights, notifications, emphasis

### Semantic Colours
- Success: Green variants
- Warning: Amber/yellow variants
- Error: Red variants
- Info: Blue variants

### Extended Palette
- Tints (+ white)
- Shades (+ black)
- Tones (+ grey)
- Used for: Hover states, disabled states, subtle variations

## Specification Standards

### Colour Documentation Format

```
Colour Name: [Descriptive name]
Role: [Primary/Secondary/Accent]
Hex: #XXXXXX
RGB: R, G, B
CMYK: C, M, Y, K
Pantone: XXXX C
HSL: H°, S%, L%
Contrast ratio on white: X.X:1
Contrast ratio on black: X.X:1
Usage notes: [When/how to use]
```

### File Format Considerations
- **Digital:** RGB, Hex
- **Print:** CMYK, Pantone
- **Large format:** Pantone matching critical
- **Merchandise:** Pantone for consistency
