## Legislative Framework

### Primary Legislation
- **Working with Children (Screening) Act 2004 (WA)** — originally titled Working with Children (Criminal Record Checking) Act 2004
- **Working with Children (Criminal Record Checking) Amendment Act 2022** — Royal Assent 7 December 2022; key amendments effective 1 July 2023

### Administering Body
Department of Communities — WWC Screening Unit

### Paramount Consideration
Section 3: The **best interests of children** are the paramount consideration in all decisions under the Act.

## Assessment Process

### Application Flow

1. Person completes application (lodged at Australia Post or online renewal)
2. WWC Screening Unit confirms identity
3. Nationally Coordinated Criminal History Check (NCCHC) requested
4. Conduct review findings checked (TRBWA, Ombudsman WA) — per s 17A(3), added by 2022 Amendment
5. Any other relevant information assessed

### Assessment Criteria (Section 12)

The CEO must consider:
- Criminal record information
- Conduct review findings or outcomes (s 17A(3))
- When the offence was committed
- Applicant's age when offence was committed
- Nature of the offence and relevance to child-related work
- Effect of future conduct on children
- Applicant's submissions and information
- Anything else reasonably relevant

### Possible Outcomes

| Outcome | Effect | Duration |
|---------|--------|----------|
| **Assessment Notice (WWC Card)** | Person cleared for child-related work | Valid 3 years |
| **Interim Negative Notice** | Immediate prohibition from child-related work; cancels any current card | Until final decision |
| **Negative Notice** | Final prohibition from child-related work | Until cancelled (can apply after 3 years) |

### Deemed Withdrawal
If requested information is not provided, the application may be deemed withdrawn and the person cannot engage in child-related work.

## Offence Classifications

### Class 1 Offences (Section 7(1), Schedule 1)

These are the most serious offences. A conviction for a Class 1 offence committed as an adult results in an **automatic Negative Notice** with no discretion.

Examples include:
- Sexual assault and sexual abuse of children
- Child cruelty
- Drug trafficking involving children
- Kidnapping or abduction of children
- Criminal Code offences involving indecent treatment of children
- Incest and bestiality where a child is involved
- Serious violence against children
- Relevant Commonwealth offences (Schedule 2 of Regulations)

### Class 2 Offences (Section 7(2), Schedule 2)

Serious but with some discretion available. A Negative Notice will be issued **unless exceptional circumstances** warrant an Assessment Notice.

Examples include:
- Assault (various degrees) where child is victim
- Grievous bodily harm involving children
- Wilful exposure to a child
- Sexual offences of lower severity than Class 1
- Relevant Commonwealth offences (Schedule 3 of Regulations)

### Class 3 Offences

Any offence not classified as Class 1 or Class 2. These are assessed on their merits — a Negative Notice may issue if the offence involves an indecent act, unless exceptional circumstances apply.

### Charges vs Convictions

Both pending charges and convictions trigger assessment. A pending charge for a Class 1 or Class 2 offence can result in a Negative Notice (or at minimum an Interim Negative Notice) before any conviction.

## Interim and Final Negative Notices

### Interim Negative Notice (Section 13)

**When issued:**
- At any point during assessment
- When information indicates **immediate risk of harm to children**
- Typically triggered by a pending serious charge or newly discovered conviction

**Effect:**
- Takes effect **immediately**
- Cancels any current WWC Card
- Person prohibited from child-related work
- Penalty for non-compliance: $60,000 fine and/or 5 years imprisonment (s 24)

**Before issuing a Final Negative Notice:**
- CEO must provide written notice of the proposal
- Person has **28 days** to make submissions
- CEO must consider any submissions received

### Final Negative Notice — Decision Framework

**Mandatory Negative Notice — no discretion (Section 12(5)):**

The CEO **must** issue a Negative Notice if aware of:
1. Conviction for Class 1 offence committed as an adult
2. Pending charge for Class 1 or Class 2 offence (unless exceptional circumstances)
3. Conviction for Class 2 offence (unless exceptional circumstances)
4. Conviction for Class 3 offence involving an indecent act (unless exceptional circumstances)

**Discretionary Negative Notice (Section 12(6)):**

The CEO **may** issue a Negative Notice where, considering all circumstances, the person poses an unacceptable risk of harm to children. Factors include:
- When the offence was committed
- Applicant's age at time of offence
- Nature and relevance to child-related work
- Effect on children
- Applicant's submissions
- Other relevant matters

## Teacher Misconduct to WWC Ban — The Nexus

This is the critical pathway introduced by the **2022 Amendment Act**.

### Section 17A — Conduct Review Authorities

Designated conduct review authorities must notify the WWC Screening Unit of findings:
1. **TRBWA** — under the Teacher Registration Act 2012
2. **Ombudsman WA** — under the Reportable Conduct Scheme

### How It Works

```
Teacher misconduct finding by TRBWA or Ombudsman
    ↓
Notification to WWC Screening Unit (s 17A)
    ↓
Automatic reassessment of WWC status (s 12(2))
    ↓
CEO applies child safety risk test
    ↓
Assessment Notice retained  OR  Negative Notice issued
```

### TRBWA Findings That Trigger WWC Reassessment

Any of the following triggers notification to the WWC Screening Unit:
- Finding of misconduct under Part 5 of the Teacher Registration Act
- Serious incompetence finding (s 48A)
- Serious misconduct finding (s 48B)
- Any Disciplinary Committee decision
- Any Impairment Review Committee decision
- Any SAT determination relating to teacher conduct

### The WWC Threshold Test

The CEO determines whether the misconduct indicates the person **"poses an unacceptable risk of harm to children."**

This is a **risk-based assessment**, not a punitive one. The question is forward-looking: given what is known about this person's conduct, would allowing them to work with children create an unacceptable risk?

### Misconduct Types and Likely WWC Outcomes

| Type of misconduct finding | WWC outcome |
|---------------------------|-------------|
| Sexual misconduct with student | Negative Notice — almost certain |
| Established grooming behaviour | Negative Notice — highly probable |
| Physical abuse of students | Negative Notice — highly probable |
| Conviction for Class 1 offence (adult) | Automatic Negative Notice — mandatory |
| Conviction for Class 2 offence | Negative Notice unless exceptional circumstances |
| Serious professional misconduct (not involving children) | Reassessment triggered; outcome depends on nature and child safety nexus |
| General incompetence (no child safety risk) | Reassessment triggered; Negative Notice unlikely |
| Administrative misconduct (fraud, dishonesty) | Reassessment triggered; Negative Notice unlikely unless children involved |
| Single corrected boundary breach | Reassessment triggered; Negative Notice unlikely |

### Key Principle

The bar for a WWC ban is **child safety**, not professional standards. A teacher can lose their TRBWA registration for professional failings without necessarily receiving a WWC Negative Notice, and conversely, conduct that doesn't meet TRBWA thresholds could still result in a Negative Notice if it reveals risk to children.

## Appeal and Review Rights

### SAT Review of WWC Decisions (Section 26)

**Time limit:** 28 days from date of Negative Notice
**Application:** Written application to SAT
**Scope:** Fresh hearing on the merits — SAT can consider new material

**Reviewable decisions:**
- Decision to issue a Negative Notice
- Decision to refuse to cancel a Negative Notice
- Decision to cancel and substitute a Negative Notice (s 14(2))

### Cancellation of Negative Notice (Section 19)

A person can apply to cancel a Negative Notice:

**After 3 years:**
- Must demonstrate circumstances have genuinely changed
- CEO considers risk mitigation

**Earlier cancellation possible if:**
- Pending charge was dismissed or person acquitted
- Information on which decision was based was incorrect or incomplete
- Circumstances genuinely changed to remove risk

### Cancellation Assessment Criteria (Section 12(8))

- When the offence was committed
- Applicant's age at time of offence
- Nature of offence and relevance to child-related work
- Effect of future conduct on children
- Applicant's submissions
- Anything else reasonably relevant

## Employer Obligations

### Before Employment Commences

1. Identify all positions as child-related employment
2. Require all employees and non-employees (volunteers, contractors) to apply for WWC Check
3. Ensure valid WWC Check before person commences child-related work
4. May allow commencement with pending application in limited circumstances

### Ongoing Compliance

1. **Record-keeping:** Maintain WWC Check details in the WWC Recording System
2. **Validation:** Regularly verify card validity (not just expiry — cards can be cancelled mid-term)
3. **Monitoring:** Track all employees, volunteers, and contractors in child-related roles

### Immediate Actions Required

| Trigger | Required action | Timeframe |
|---------|----------------|-----------|
| Employee issued Interim Negative Notice | Remove from school site immediately | Immediately |
| Employee issued Negative Notice | Remove from child-related work | Immediately |
| Relevant change in employee's criminal record (Class 1/2 charge or conviction) | Notify WWC Screening Unit | As soon as practicable |
| Teacher dismissed/suspended for misconduct | Notify TRBWA (s 42, Teacher Registration Act) | Within 7 days |

### Penalties for Non-Compliance

| Offence | Penalty |
|---------|---------|
| Engaging person with Interim/Negative Notice in child-related work (s 24) | $60,000 fine and/or 5 years imprisonment |
| Employing person with Class 1 conviction without valid Assessment Notice (s 32) | $60,000 fine and/or 5 years imprisonment |
| Failure to provide information requested by CEO (s 34) | Penalties apply |

## 2022 Amendment Act — Key Changes

The Working with Children (Criminal Record Checking) Amendment Act 2022 substantially strengthened the regime. Key changes effective 1 July 2023:

1. **Broadened offence classes** — increased number of prescribed Class 1 and Class 2 offences
2. **Added Section 17A** — conduct review findings from TRBWA and Ombudsman now trigger automatic reassessment
3. **Conduct review information** — CEO must check conduct review findings before deciding applications (not just criminal records)
4. **Removed volunteer exemptions** — persons with Interim/Negative Notices can no longer access parent or child volunteer exemptions
5. **Enhanced compliance powers** — WWC Screening Unit authorised officers have powers of entry, search, and inspection (with warrants)
6. **Strengthened information sharing** — improved data flow between TRBWA, Ombudsman, and WWC Screening Unit

### Significance for Teacher Misconduct

The 2022 amendments closed a significant gap. Previously, a teacher could lose their TRBWA registration for serious misconduct but retain their WWC Card because there was no automatic notification pathway. Now, any TRBWA disciplinary finding triggers reassessment, ensuring the WWC system captures conduct-based risks alongside criminal history.
