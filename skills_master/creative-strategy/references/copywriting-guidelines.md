# Copywriting Guidelines

## Headline Craft

### Headline Formulas

**Benefit-Led:**
"[Outcome] without [Pain Point]"
→ "Professional results without the learning curve"

**Challenge:**
"Are you still [outdated approach]?"
→ "Are you still scheduling posts manually?"

**Social Proof:**
"[Number] [audience] already [action]"
→ "10,000 creators already switched"

**How-To:**
"How to [achieve outcome] in [timeframe]"
→ "How to launch your brand in a weekend"

**Question:**
"What if [desirable scenario]?"
→ "What if your workflow actually worked?"

**Contrast:**
"[Old way] vs [New way]"
→ "Stress vs. Strategy"

### Headline Rules
- 8 words maximum for primary headlines
- Front-load the benefit
- Use active verbs
- One idea per headline
- Test multiple options

## Body Copy Architecture

### Paragraph Structure
- 2-3 sentences maximum per paragraph
- One idea per paragraph
- Varied sentence length creates rhythm
- Start strong, end stronger

### Rhythm Patterns

**Short-medium-short:**
Quick hook. Then expand with supporting detail that builds on the initial thought. Land it.

**Building:**
Start simply. Then add complexity as the reader commits. Layer in proof points. Create momentum toward the conclusion that ties everything together.

**Punchy:**
Direct. Clear. Memorable.

## CTA Framework

### CTA Formula
[Action verb] + [Value/Outcome]

**Strong CTAs:**
- Start creating
- Get your free guide
- Join the community
- See it in action
- Book your spot

**Weak CTAs:**
- Submit
- Click here
- Learn more (overused)
- Sign up (too generic)
- Send

### CTA Context Matching

| Funnel Stage | CTA Tone | Example |
|--------------|----------|---------|
| Awareness | Low commitment | "See how it works" |
| Consideration | Value-focused | "Get the comparison guide" |
| Decision | Action-oriented | "Start your trial" |
| Loyalty | Community | "Share your story" |

## Long-Form Content

### Blog Post Structure
```
Hook (1-2 sentences): Capture attention
Context (1 paragraph): Why this matters now
Promise (1 sentence): What reader will gain
Body (sectioned): Deliver on promise
Takeaway: Single actionable insight
CTA: Relevant next step
```

### Content Principles
- Earn every sentence
- Cut ruthlessly
- Use subheadings as signposts
- Examples > explanations
- Link to deeper resources

## Email Copywriting

### Subject Line Formulas

**Curiosity gap:**
"The [topic] secret nobody talks about"

**Direct benefit:**
"Your [outcome] starts here"

**Personal:**
"[Name], quick question"

**Urgency (use sparingly):**
"Last chance: [offer]"

**Preview text:** Extend the subject line, don't repeat it.

### Email Body Structure
- One clear objective per email
- Scannable formatting
- Single CTA (repeated 2-3x max)
- PS lines for secondary messages

## Social Media Copy

### Platform-Specific Guidelines

**Instagram:**
- Caption hooks in first line (before "more")
- Conversational, personal tone
- Strategic hashtag use (5-15)
- CTAs feel native, not pushy

**TikTok:**
- Captions support video, don't duplicate
- Trending sounds/hashtags where authentic
- Community language and references

**LinkedIn:**
- Opening line = scroll-stopper
- Thought leadership positioning
- Generous with insights
- Engagement-driving questions

**Twitter/X:**
- Punchy, quotable
- Thread structure for longer content
- Personality forward

## Voice Consistency Checks

### Before Publishing Checklist
- [ ] Does this sound like us?
- [ ] Would we say this to a customer's face?
- [ ] Is every sentence earning its place?
- [ ] Are we being clear, not clever for clever's sake?
- [ ] Does the CTA feel natural?

### Red Flags
- Jargon creep
- Passive voice overuse
- Hedge words (maybe, probably, potentially)
- Multiple competing messages
- Feature focus without benefit translation

## Australian English Specifics

### Spelling
- -ise endings: organise, realise, recognise
- -our endings: colour, favour, behaviour
- -re endings: centre, metre, theatre
- -l doubling: travelling, cancelled

### Punctuation
- Single quotes for quotations
- Logical punctuation (commas outside quotes)
- Date format: 12 December 2024 or 12/12/24

### Terminology
- Footpath (not sidewalk)
- Petrol (not gas)
- Mobile (not cell)
- Uni (not college)
- Mum (not mom)

### Tone
- Self-deprecating humour lands well
- Excessive enthusiasm feels American
- Directness valued over formality
- Swearing context-dependent but not taboo
