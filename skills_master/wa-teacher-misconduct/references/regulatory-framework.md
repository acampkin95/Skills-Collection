## Teacher Registration Act 2012

### Paramount Principle (Section 5)

All persons and bodies performing functions under the Act must regard the **best interests of children as the paramount consideration**. This overarching principle guides every assessment, investigation, and determination.

### Key Definitions (Section 3)

**Registered teacher:** A person who holds registration under Part 3 of the Act.

**Formerly registered teacher:** A person whose registration has been cancelled, expired, or surrendered.

**Serious offence:** Defined by reference to prescribed offences including sexual offences, offences against children, and other prescribed categories.

**Actionable offence:** Includes offences resulting in child sex offender status, sexual offences committed against or in respect of a child, sexual offences where a child was present, and offences involving child sexual abuse material.

**Impairment:** One or more conditions arising from birth defect, illness, or injury — including defects in body/brain structure or functioning, illness impairing thought processes, perception of reality, emotions, or judgment.

## Misconduct Categories

### Disciplinary Matters (Section 47)

A disciplinary matter exists when a registered or formerly registered teacher has:

**(a) Contravened the Act**
Teaching without valid registration, failing to notify TRBWA of relevant changes, contravening any provision.

**(b) Contravened registration conditions**
Breach of any condition imposed on registration — may relate to professional development, supervision requirements, curriculum restrictions, or student contact limitations.

**(c) Contravened Board/Committee/Tribunal orders**
Failure to comply with orders made under Part 5 of the Act.

**(d) Criminal convictions**
Conviction or finding of guilt for:
- (i) Serious offence (s 3 definition)
- (ii) Actionable offence — including child sex offender status, sexual offences against/in respect of children, sexual offences where child present, child sexual abuse material offences
- (iii) Prescribed offence (per regulations)

**(e) Serious incompetence (Section 48A)**
Teaching at a standard substantially below that reasonably expected, assessed by:
- Frequency of the incompetent conduct
- Extent of the incompetence
- Risks caused to students or others
- Teacher's training, qualifications, and experience level

**(f) Serious misconduct (Section 48B)**
Improper conduct of a serious kind that departs from the standard of behaviour reasonably expected of a registered teacher. This is a broad standard assessed contextually.

**(g) Contravention of undertakings**
Breach of any undertaking given to the Board under the Act.

**(h) Teaching while suspended**
Teaching at an educational institution while registration is suspended.

### Serious Misconduct — Examples by Category

**Sexual misconduct:**
- Sexual contact with students (any form)
- Sexual communication (verbal, written, digital)
- Grooming behaviour (progressive boundary erosion aimed at sexual exploitation)
- Exposure of genitals or sexual material to students
- Sexual comments, jokes, or innuendo directed at students
- Possession or distribution of child sexual abuse material
- Sexual relationship with student (including adult students where teacher-student relationship exists)

**Physical misconduct:**
- Physical assault of students beyond reasonable force for safety/restraint
- Excessive physical discipline
- Reckless acts endangering student safety
- Use of weapons or dangerous implements near students

**Emotional/psychological misconduct:**
- Persistent bullying, intimidation, or humiliation of students
- Deliberate psychological manipulation
- Creating a hostile or threatening learning environment
- Using position to coerce or control students

**Professional misconduct:**
- Fraud, dishonesty, or theft in professional capacity
- Falsifying qualifications, records, or assessments
- Substance abuse affecting professional duties
- Serious breach of confidentiality
- Financial impropriety involving students or school funds

**Safeguarding failures:**
- Failure to report suspected child abuse (when mandated)
- Failure to act on known risks to student safety
- Concealing misconduct by colleagues
- Deliberate circumvention of child protection protocols

### Impairment Matters (Section 48)

An impairment matter exists when a teacher's ability to carry out inherent teaching requirements is affected by impairment. The impairment pathway is supportive rather than purely punitive — it focuses on health assessment and management rather than discipline.

However, impairment does not excuse misconduct. Where conduct involves both impairment and misconduct elements, both pathways may run concurrently.

## Disciplinary Committee Process

### Composition
Disciplinary Committees must include an **Australian lawyer** and are appointed by the TRBWA Board to hear specific matters.

### Complaint Pathways (Sections 51-57)

**Public complaints (s 51):** Any person may make a written complaint to TRBWA about a teacher's conduct.

**Board-formulated complaints (s 51A):** The Board itself can formulate a complaint based on information it receives through notifications, investigations, or other means.

### Assessment Phase (Section 63)
The Disciplinary Committee conducts initial assessment to determine whether the complaint warrants a formal inquiry.

### Inquiry Phase (Sections 64-67)

**Teacher's rights during inquiry:**
- Present evidence and call witnesses (s 65)
- Be represented by a legal practitioner or other representative (s 67)
- Cross-examine witnesses
- Respond to all allegations (show cause)
- Have evidence from other proceedings considered (s 66)

**Standard of proof:** Balance of probabilities — the allegation must be shown to be "more likely than not" to have occurred. This is lower than the criminal standard of "beyond reasonable doubt."

**Evidence rules:** The Committee is not bound by strict rules of evidence but must observe procedural fairness.

### Interim Disciplinary Orders (Section 59)

Where there is an immediate risk, TRBWA can impose interim orders including:
- Suspension of registration
- Conditions on registration
- These must be referred to SAT within **14 days**

## Disciplinary Outcomes and Sanctions

### Range of Sanctions (Section 69)

Following inquiry, the Disciplinary Committee may impose:

| Sanction | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Caution** | Formal warning recorded on register |
| **Reprimand** | More serious formal censure recorded on register |
| **Fine** | Maximum $5,000 |
| **Conditions on registration** | May include: mandatory supervision, professional development, restricted student contact, mentoring, health monitoring |
| **Suspension** | Maximum 2 years; registration suspended but not cancelled |
| **Cancellation** | Registration cancelled; teacher can no longer teach |
| **Disqualification** | Teacher prohibited from applying for registration for a specified period |
| **Cost orders** | Teacher may be ordered to pay investigation/hearing costs |

### Factors Affecting Sanction Severity

- Nature and seriousness of the conduct
- Whether students were harmed or placed at risk
- Whether the conduct was a single incident or pattern
- Teacher's insight and remorse
- Cooperation with investigation
- Prior disciplinary history
- Impact on public confidence in the profession
- Whether the teacher has taken remedial steps
- Length of service and professional reputation
- Any mitigating personal circumstances

### Substitution of Sanctions

TRBWA or SAT may substitute one form of sanction for another — for example, cancelling registration with substitution of a period of suspension with conditions.

### Publication (Sections 117-118)

The Board must balance public accountability with privacy. Findings may be published, though identifying details may be suppressed in some cases.

## State Administrative Tribunal Review

### Jurisdiction

SAT handles the most serious teacher misconduct matters. Cases reach SAT through:
- **Direct referral by TRBWA** for matters too serious for the Disciplinary Committee
- **Referral on recommendation** of a Disciplinary Committee
- **Appeal/review** of TRBWA disciplinary decisions

### SAT Process

- **Fresh hearing on the merits** — SAT conducts a complete rehearing, not just a review of the original decision
- **Not bound by rules of evidence** or court procedures
- **Can consider new material** not available at the original hearing
- **De novo examination** — considers the matter afresh

### SAT Powers

If SAT finds a disciplinary matter exists, it may order:
- Suspension of registration (specified period)
- Cancellation of registration
- Disqualification from applying for registration (specified period)
- Imposition of conditions
- Modification of existing orders
- Any combination of the above

### SAT Interlocutory Powers

SAT can impose interlocutory (interim) orders including immediate suspension pending final determination.

### Appeal from SAT

SAT decisions can be appealed to the **Supreme Court of Western Australia** on questions of law.

### Published Decisions

SAT publishes decisions (sometimes with names removed) via the SAT eCourts Portal: sat.justice.wa.gov.au

For statistics, use the latest published TRBWA annual report and quote the reporting year. Earlier notes identified SAT referral and sanction data in the 2022-23/2023-24 reporting period, but those figures should not be treated as current without checking the latest report.

## Australian Professional Standards for Teachers

The TRBWA has adopted the APST under Section 20 of the Act. These standards define what teachers should know and be able to do at four career stages.

### Three Domains, Seven Standards

**Domain 1 — Professional Knowledge:**
- Standard 1: Know students and how they learn
- Standard 2: Know the content and how to teach it

**Domain 2 — Professional Practice:**
- Standard 3: Plan for and implement effective teaching and learning
- Standard 4: Create and maintain supportive and safe learning environments
- Standard 5: Assess, provide feedback and report on student learning

**Domain 3 — Professional Engagement:**
- Standard 6: Engage in professional learning
- Standard 7: Engage professionally with colleagues, parents/carers and the community

### Career Stages

- **Graduate:** Entry-level standard for initial registration
- **Proficient:** Required for full registration
- **Highly Accomplished:** Advanced career recognition
- **Lead:** Highest career stage recognition

### Relevance to Misconduct Assessment

The APST provides the baseline against which professional competence is measured. Departure from these standards informs assessments of both serious incompetence (s 48A) and, where the departure involves conduct rather than competence, serious misconduct (s 48B).

## Fit and Proper Person Test

### Section 24 — Registration Requirement

To be registered, a teacher must satisfy the Board that they are a **fit and proper person** to be a registered teacher. This assessment applies at initial registration, renewal, and can be revisited at any time.

### Assessment Factors

The Board considers:
- Criminal history (WA and national)
- Disciplinary history in any jurisdiction (Australian or international)
- Whether the person has been found guilty of an actionable offence
- Character references and professional history
- Any other matter the Board considers relevant to fitness to teach

### Ongoing Obligation

The fit and proper person requirement is not a one-time threshold. Conduct after registration that would have prevented registration in the first place can trigger disciplinary proceedings and potential cancellation.
