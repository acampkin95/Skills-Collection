<process>
## Phase 1: Discovery & Research

### Brand Foundation Questions
1. **Purpose**: Why does this brand exist? What change do they want to create?
2. **Values**: What principles guide their decisions?
3. **Personality**: If the brand were a person, how would you describe them in 3-4 traits?
4. **Positioning**: How are they different from competitors?
5. **Audience**: Who are they serving? (Use audience-personas.md framework)

### Competitive Analysis
- Map 5-8 key competitors
- Analyze their visual identity systems
- Identify white space opportunities
- Note overused visual trends to avoid

## Phase 2: Visual Identity Development

### Step 1: Colourway Development
Follow the colourway development framework:
- **Primary palette**: 2-3 hero colours (60% usage)
- **Secondary palette**: 2-3 supporting colours (30% usage)
- **Accent palette**: 1-2 pop colours for CTAs/highlights (10% usage)

**Distinctive strategies:**
- Duotone contrasts with unexpected pairings
- Gradient systems that feel organic, not generic
- Muted-meets-saturated tension
- Warm/cool push-pull dynamics
- Cultural colour associations (research target audience meanings)

**Accessibility check**: Ensure WCAG AA compliance (4.5:1 contrast ratio minimum)

### Step 2: Typography System
**Type Selection Criteria:**
- **Serif**: Heritage, authority, timeless elegance
- **Sans Serif**: Modern, clear, approachable
- **Display**: Distinctive moments, campaign heroes

**Pairing Principles:**
- Contrast without conflict (weight, style, proportion)
- Superfamily harmony or historical pairing
- Never more than 2-3 typefaces per brand system

**Hierarchy Development:**
- H1-H6 heading scales
- Body text sizes and line heights
- Caption and label sizes
- Button and UI text specifications

### Step 3: Logo System Design
**Logo Components:**
- Primary logo (horizontal)
- Stacked logo (vertical)
- Icon/mark only
- Wordmark only
- Minimum size specifications
- Clear space requirements

**Logo Structure (SVG):**
```xml
<svg viewBox="0 0 200 60" role="img" aria-label="Brand">
  <title>Brand Name Logo</title>
  <g id="logo-mark"><!-- Symbol --></g>
  <g id="logo-wordmark"><!-- Text --></g>
</svg>
```

## Phase 3: Brand Guidelines Documentation

### Required Sections
Use the brand-guidelines-template.md structure:

1. **Brand Story & Purpose**
2. **Logo System & Clear Space**
3. **Colour System** (with accessibility ratios)
4. **Typography System** (scale, pairing, usage)
5. **Illustration Style Guide**
6. **Photography Direction**
7. **Voice & Tone Guidelines**
8. **Motion & Animation Principles**
9. **Application Examples**
10. **Don'ts Gallery**

### Voice & Tone Guidelines
Use the Voice Architecture Template:
```
Brand Personality: [3-4 human traits]
Tone Spectrum: [Range from formal to casual, serious to playful]
Language DNA: [Signature words, phrases, rhythms]
Forbidden Territory: [What we never say or sound like]
```

## Phase 4: Application & Testing

### Brand Applications
Design the logo and identity applied to:
- Business cards
- Letterhead
- Website headers
- Social media profiles
- Product packaging (if applicable)
- Signage

### LGBTQ+ & Inclusivity Check
Ensure the brand identity follows inclusive principles:
- ✅ Authentic representation in imagery
- ✅ Accessible colour combinations
- ✅ Inclusive language in copy
- ✅ Community-first approach
- ✅ Intersectionality consideration

### Final Review
- Does the identity feel distinctive and memorable?
- Is it scalable across all touchpoints?
- Does it resonate with the target audience (AU/Western 20-35)?
- Is it inclusive and community-focused?
- Can it evolve as the brand grows?
</process>

<success_criteria>
Brand identity development is complete when:
- All visual elements work cohesively as a system
- Brand guidelines document is comprehensive and actionable
- Identity is distinctive within the competitive landscape
- Accessibility standards are met for all colour combinations
- Voice and visual elements authentically reflect brand values
- All applications demonstrate consistent execution
- Inclusive design principles are embedded throughout
</success_criteria>
