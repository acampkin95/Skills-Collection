# Inclusive Marketing Guidelines

## Core Principles

### 1. Authentic Representation
Diversity isn't decoration—it's reality. Represent diverse identities because they exist, not for optics.

### 2. Community-First Approach
Nothing about us without us. Consult communities you're representing. Pay for that expertise.

### 3. Year-Round Commitment
Pride month visibility means nothing without year-round action. Avoid rainbow-washing.

### 4. Intersectionality
Identities overlap. A person isn't just queer OR disabled OR of colour—they may be all. Represent complexity.

### 5. Action Over Aesthetics
Rainbow logos without policy change is hollow. Back representation with tangible support.

## LGBTQ+ Marketing Guidelines

### Language Best Practices

**Use:**
- LGBTQ+ or LGBTQIA+ (abbreviation inclusive of spectrum)
- Partner, spouse, significant other (vs assuming married/boyfriend/girlfriend)
- They/them as default singular pronoun
- "Folks," "everyone," "y'all" (gender-neutral groups)
- "All genders" rather than "both genders"
- Current community-preferred terminology

**Avoid:**
- "Sexual preference" (use "sexual orientation")
- "Lifestyle" to describe identity
- Deadnaming or misgendering
- "Born a man/woman" (use "assigned male/female at birth")
- Dated terms unless reclaimed by individual

### Representation Guidelines

**Authentic portrayal:**
- Show LGBTQ+ people in everyday contexts, not just Pride
- Diverse relationships, families, life stages
- Range of gender expressions
- Intersectional representation (QTBIPOC, disabled queer folks, etc.)

**Avoid:**
- Tokenism (single queer character/image as checkbox)
- Trauma-focused narratives only
- Coming out as only queer story
- Stereotypical casting or depiction
- Queer-coding villains or problematic characters

### Campaign Considerations

**Before launching LGBTQ+ targeted campaigns:**
- Review with community members/consultants
- Ensure organisation policies align with messaging
- Confirm charitable commitments if claiming them
- Consider community partnerships over corporate solo efforts

## Gender-Inclusive Guidelines

### Default Assumptions
- Use gender-neutral language as default
- Avoid gendered assumptions in scenarios
- Include non-binary options in forms
- Don't assume gender based on name/appearance

### Pronouns
- Normalise sharing pronouns (in bios, introductions)
- Respect stated pronouns without question
- Use singular "they" when unsure
- Update systems to support diverse pronouns

### Gendered Products/Services
- Question whether gendering is necessary
- If gendering, include non-binary option
- Avoid reinforcing stereotypes in gendered offerings

## Cultural Diversity Guidelines

### Representation Standards
- Reflect actual demographic diversity
- Include diverse backgrounds in everyday scenarios
- Represent variety within cultural groups (not monolithic)
- Feature multicultural families and relationships

### Avoiding Harm
- No stereotyping or single-story representations
- Research cultural context before using cultural elements
- Credit and compensate cultural sources
- Avoid appropriation vs appreciation (when in doubt, ask)

### Practical Steps
- Diverse creative teams produce diverse work
- Community review for cultural depictions
- Multilingual considerations where appropriate
- Cultural calendar awareness for campaign timing

## Accessibility in Marketing

### Visual Content
- Alt text for all images
- Colour contrast meeting WCAG standards
- No reliance on colour alone for meaning
- Captions for video content
- Audio descriptions where possible

### Written Content
- Plain language principles
- Clear hierarchy and structure
- Sans-serif fonts for digital
- Adequate text sizing

### Representation
- Include disabled people in imagery (authentically, not as inspiration)
- Diverse disabilities represented (not just wheelchair users)
- Disabled people in everyday scenarios, not just disability-focused
- Disabled creators and consultants in production

---

## Body Diversity

### Representation Standards
- Range of body sizes in imagery
- Diverse abilities and mobility
- Various ages within target demographics
- Avoid digital alteration that homogenises bodies

### Language Guidelines
- Avoid weight-based language or assumptions
- No "before/after" framing that demonises bodies
- Focus on how products work, not body "improvement"
- Celebrate function over aesthetics where appropriate

---

## Age-Inclusive Marketing

### Avoiding Ageism
- Don't equate age with capability
- Represent ages authentically (not just young or stereotypically old)
- Avoid "anti-aging" framing
- Include multiple generations in content

### Generational Considerations
- Avoid generational stereotyping (not all Gen Z are TikTok natives)
- Research actual behaviours vs assumed preferences
- Accessible design serves all ages

---

## Socioeconomic Considerations

### Inclusive Positioning
- Don't assume financial circumstances
- Offer value at multiple price points where possible
- Avoid language that assumes wealth ("treat yourself" for expensive items)
- Consider geographic economic variation

### Representation
- Include diverse economic circumstances in imagery
- Show product/service in various life contexts
- Avoid exclusively aspirational/wealthy settings

### Before Publishing

**Representation:**
- [ ] Diverse identities represented authentically
- [ ] Avoiding tokenism and stereotypes
- [ ] Community consultation completed
- [ ] Intersectionality considered

**Language:**
- [ ] Gender-neutral defaults
- [ ] Current, respectful terminology
- [ ] Assumptions questioned
- [ ] Accessible reading level

**Accessibility:**
- [ ] Alt text complete
- [ ] Captions provided
- [ ] Colour contrast verified
- [ ] Screen reader tested

**Alignment:**
- [ ] Company policies match messaging
- [ ] Team diversity reflects values
- [ ] Action supports aesthetics
- [ ] Long-term commitment evident
