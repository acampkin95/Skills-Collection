# Evidence Standards — WA Magistrates Court

> Load this file when helping format affidavits, organise evidence bundles, or prepare documents for hearing.

## Affidavit Requirements

### Which Form to Use

| Context | Form | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Civil — general evidence | **CIV Form 2** | General Form of Affidavit |
| Civil — personal statement | **CIV Form 11** | Affidavit of Self (prescribed headings) |
| Civil — document disclosure | **CIV Form 35** | Verifying Disclosure of Documents |
| Criminal — any application | **CPR Form 2** | General Affidavit |

### Formatting Rules

1. **Prescribed headings:** Use the court-provided template headings exactly
2. **Numbered paragraphs:** Each paragraph consecutively numbered
3. **One fact per paragraph:** Keep each paragraph to a single factual point
4. **Chronological order:** Where possible, present facts in date order
5. **Source of knowledge:** Clearly state how you know each fact:
   - "I personally observed…" (direct knowledge)
   - "I was told by [Name] on [Date] that…" (hearsay — identify source)
   - "I believe… and the grounds for my belief are…" (belief — state basis)
6. **Language:** Use "I say that…", "I know…", "I believe…", "I was told…"
7. **Avoid:** Opinion (except expert), argument, emotion, legal conclusions
8. **Exhibits:** Referenced as "Exhibit A", "Exhibit B" etc. — attached as separate pages
9. **Signature:** Sign at the end — **do NOT sign before the swearing ceremony**
10. **Jurat:** Completed by the authorised witness at the swearing ceremony

### Swearing/Affirming Process

1. Bring original unsigned affidavit, photo ID, and all exhibits
2. Authorised witness reviews form compliance
3. Read document (aloud or silently — officer confirms)
4. Choose oath (religious) or affirmation (secular)
5. Sign in front of authorised witness
6. Authorised witness completes jurat section
7. Receive sealed copy — this is the official version for filing

**Authorised witnesses:** Justice of the Peace, Commissioner for Oaths, legal practitioner, notary public

### Objecting to an Affidavit

- Use **CIV Form 58** (Notice of Objection to Affidavit Evidence)
- Lodge and serve as soon as practicable after receiving the affidavit
- Common grounds: hearsay, irrelevance, improper opinion, lack of qualification

### Court Limitation

The court **cannot** help prepare affidavits. If assistance is needed, seek help from Legal Aid WA, a community legal centre, or a private lawyer.

## Evidence Bundle Organisation

### Structure for Trial or Hearing

```
COVER PAGE
├── Case Name & File Number
├── Prepared by: [Your Name]
├── Date Prepared: [YYYY-MM-DD]
└── "Bundle of Documents — [Claimant/Defendant]"

INDEX (unnumbered pages before document section)
├── Document 1: Affidavit of [Name] .................. pages 1–15
├── Document 2: Contract dated [Date] ................ pages 16–22
├── Document 3: Email correspondence ................. pages 23–45
└── Document 4: Invoices ............................. pages 46–60

DOCUMENTS (consecutively paginated from page 1)
├── [Tab] Affidavit 1 ................................ pages 1–15
├── [Tab] Contract ................................... pages 16–22
├── [Tab] Emails (chronological) ..................... pages 23–45
└── [Tab] Invoices (chronological) ................... pages 46–60
```

### Pagination Rules

- Number every page in centre footer: `-1-`, `-2-`, etc.
- Consecutive numbering through entire bundle (not per document)
- Index on unnumbered pages before the paginated documents
- PDF tools (Adobe Acrobat, macOS Preview) can add page numbers

### Copies Required

Prepare **identical** copies for:
1. The court (magistrate)
2. The opposing party
3. Yourself (working copy)

### Physical Preparation

- A4 paper, single-sided printing preferred
- Left margin at least 3cm for court registry binding
- Use tabs or dividers for major sections
- Bind or staple securely
- Font: minimum 12pt (Times New Roman or Arial recommended)
- Line spacing: single or 1.5

### Evidence Organisation Principles

- **Chronological order** within each category
- **Group by type:** Affidavits together, correspondence together, financial documents together
- **Reference system:** Refer to page numbers when speaking to the magistrate
- **Relevance:** Include only documents relevant to the issues in dispute
- **Originals:** Bring originals to court; file copies with the court

## Document Format Requirements (WA Courts)

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Paper size | A4 (210mm × 297mm) |
| Font size | Minimum 12pt |
| Font type | Times New Roman or Arial recommended |
| Margins | Minimum 2.5cm all sides; 3cm left for binding |
| Line spacing | Single or 1.5 |
| Paragraphs | Consecutively numbered |
| Pages | Consecutively numbered (centre footer) |
| Filing format | PDF preferred for eLodgment |

## Exhibit Marking

### How to Mark Exhibits

- Each exhibit gets a unique letter: Exhibit A, Exhibit B, etc.
- Attach exhibit certificate/sticker to front of each exhibit:
  ```
  This is the exhibit marked "[A]" referred to in the
  affidavit of [Deponent Name] sworn/affirmed on [Date].

  Before me: _______________
  [Authorised witness signature]
  ```
- Reference in affidavit body: "Annexed hereto and marked Exhibit A is a copy of…"

### Exhibit Best Practices

- Keep exhibits in the same order as referenced in the affidavit
- Do not alter or annotate original exhibits
- If an exhibit is lengthy, include only relevant pages with a note about what was excluded
- For electronic communications (emails, SMS): include headers showing sender, recipient, date/time

## Electronic Evidence Standards

WA Magistrates Court accepts electronic evidence subject to format requirements. See [Electronic Evidentiary Material Guide (PDF)](https://www.magistratescourt.wa.gov.au/_files/Electronic_Evidentiary_Material.pdf).

### File Format Requirements
- All PC-based files must be **compatible with Windows 10**
- Document camera available in most courtrooms for displaying documents on screen
- Video evidence must be in a standard format playable on court equipment

### Presenting Electronic Evidence at Trial
- Notify court in advance if you intend to use electronic evidence
- Bring evidence on USB drive as backup
- If using eTrials (available in CLC Perth courtrooms): exhibits are entered into ICMS database and linked in transcripts
- Request a directions hearing to arrange eTrial logistics if needed

### Screenshots and Digital Communications
- Include full metadata: sender, recipient, date, time, platform
- Screenshot entire conversation threads (not individual messages out of context)
- Preserve original files — do not crop or edit screenshots used as evidence
- For social media: include profile identification and URL where possible
