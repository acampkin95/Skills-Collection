# WA Magistrates Court — Criminal Procedures Reference

> Load this file when the user's case is in the criminal division of the WA Magistrates Court.

## Offence Categories

### Simple Offences (Summary Jurisdiction)
- **Exclusive** Magistrates Court jurisdiction for adult simple offences
- **Examples:** Minor traffic offences, low-level assaults, theft under threshold, disorderly conduct
- **Process:** Streamlined; plea entered (guilty/not guilty); trial conducted by magistrate
- **Time limit:** Generally **12 months** from date of offence to lay charges

### Indictable Offences Heard Summarily
- Less serious indictable offences where accused and/or prosecutor agree to summary hearing
- Heard in Magistrates Court rather than District Court
- Subject to Magistrates Court sentencing limits
- Under Criminal Procedure Act 2004 s.39

### Traffic Matters
- Road Traffic Act 1974 (WA) offences
- **Examples:** DUI, licence offences, vehicle regulation breaches
- **Sentences:** Fines, licence disqualification, conditional discharges, community-based orders

## Bail Application Procedure

### When Bail is Considered
- After arrest, accused has right to bail hearing
- Can apply at first appearance or any subsequent hearing
- Use CPR Form 6 (General Application)

### Supporting Materials
- Affidavit (CPR Form 2) outlining circumstances, ties to community, employment
- Character references (2–3 preferred)
- Evidence of residential stability
- Employment confirmation
- Surety information (CPR Form 9) if offering surety

### Character References — Requirements
- **Addressee:** "The Presiding Magistrate"
- **Format:** Typed A4 preferred (one page ideal); neat handwriting accepted
- **Must be:** Dated, signed, and recent
- **Content:** Specific examples of positive conduct, personal relationship with accused
- **Suitable referees:** Employers, teachers, doctors, community leaders, family friends, coaches
- **Impact:** Read before sentencing; significant influence on sentence
- **DO NOT** include: opinions on guilt/innocence, legal arguments, or criticisms of complainant

## First Appearance and Plea

### First Appearance
- Accused informed of charges
- Plea **not required** at first hearing
- Adjournment usually granted to obtain legal advice
- Duty lawyer may be available (contact court on the day)

### Plea Options
- **Guilty:** Proceed to sentencing (with mitigation)
- **Not guilty:** Matter set for trial
- **Adjournment:** Seek more time for legal advice or preparation

### Guilty Plea — Mitigation Materials
1. CPR Form 2 affidavit explaining circumstances
2. Character references (lodge at time of guilty plea)
3. Evidence of rehabilitation (counselling, courses, community service)
4. Prior record (court will have this; be prepared to address it)

### Not Guilty — Trial Preparation
1. Prosecution provides brief of evidence / statement of facts (disclosure)
2. Accused prepares response, identifies defence witnesses
3. Arrange subpoenas if needed for witnesses or documents
4. Court schedules trial date

## Adjournment Procedures

### Requesting an Adjournment
- **Common reasons:** Obtain legal advice, gather documents, arrange witnesses
- **Who can request:** Accused, duty lawyer, prosecution
- **Consent adjournments:** Both parties agree — streamlined approval
- **Contested adjournments:** Magistrate's discretion

### Practice Direction 4 of 2020 (Consent Adjournments)
- Requirements for agreed adjournments
- Notification requirements and procedural steps
- [Practice Direction 4 of 2020 (PDF)](https://www.magistratescourt.wa.gov.au/_files/Practice_Direction_4_of_2020.pdf)

### Practice Direction 5 of 2020 (Disclosure/Committal Hearings)
- Historically required some applications to be emailed to Perth Magistrates Court no later than 4pm the day before the listed event; verify the current practice direction, registry email, and any later direction before using this deadline.
- Applies to disclosure and committal hearing adjournments
- [Practice Direction 5 of 2020 (PDF)](https://magistratescourt.wa.gov.au/_files/Practice_Direction_5_of_2020.pdf)

### Practice Direction 2 of 2022 (Mandated eLodgment of Prosecution Notices)
- Prosecution notices must be lodged via Electronic Case Management System
- Relevant for accused receiving prosecution-filed documents electronically

### Practice Direction 1 of 2022 (Section 26 Criminal Procedure Act)
- Covers s.26 CPA 2004 provisions
- Verify the current practice direction text and site update date before relying on this procedure.

### Practice Direction 3 of 2021 (Electronic Devices in Court)
- Governs use of laptops, tablets, phones in courtroom
- SRLs may use devices to access documents during proceedings — subject to magistrate's permission

## Critical Deadlines — Criminal

| Action | Timeframe | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Charge commencement (simple offence) | **12 months** from offence | General rule under Criminal Procedure Act 2004 |
| Adjournment application (disclosure/committal) | Verify current practice direction | Historically 4pm day prior for some PD 5/2020 matters; confirm before relying |
| Plea at first appearance | **Not required** | Adjournment usually granted |
| Prosecution disclosure | **Before trial** | Timing per prosecution |
| Bail hearing | **Within prescribed timeframe** | Varies; Bail Act 1982 |

## Sentencing Options (Magistrates Court)

When a guilty plea is entered or a guilty verdict returned, the magistrate may impose:

| Sentence Type | Description | Notes |
|---------------|-------------|-------|
| **Fine** | Monetary penalty | Instalments available; enforcement via Fines Enforcement Registry |
| **Conditional Release Order (CRO)** | Good behaviour bond with conditions | 6–24 months; breach triggers re-sentencing |
| **Community-Based Order (CBO)** | Supervision, community service, programs | Up to 2 years; includes reporting obligations |
| **Intensive Supervision Order (ISO)** | Structured supervision with conditions | Alternative to imprisonment for eligible offences |
| **Suspended Imprisonment** | Prison term suspended for set period | Activated if further offences during suspension |
| **Imprisonment** | Custodial sentence | Magistrate limit: generally 2 years single offence, 5 years aggregate |
| **Spent Conviction** | Conviction not disclosed after qualifying period | Less serious offences; Spent Convictions Act 1988 |

**Mitigating factors:** Early guilty plea, good character, remorse, rehabilitation steps, no prior record, personal circumstances.

**Aggravating factors:** Prior record, breach of trust, planned conduct, victim impact, offence committed on bail.
