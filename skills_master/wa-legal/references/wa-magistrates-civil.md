# WA Magistrates Court — Civil Procedures Reference

> Load this file when the user's case is in the civil division of the WA Magistrates Court.

## Claim Types and Thresholds

### Minor Case Procedure (CIV Form 4)
- **Jurisdiction:** Claims for debt or damages up to **$10,000**
- **Key features:**
  - Informal, settlement-focused
  - Legal representation NOT permitted unless all parties and magistrate agree
  - Proceedings held in private
  - Successful party entitled to allowable costs only (not full legal costs)
- **If claimant elects general procedure** for a ≤$10,000 claim: claimant may not recover full legal costs even if they win

### General Procedure Claim (CIV Form 3)
- **Jurisdiction:** Claims for debt or damages **$10,001 to $75,000**
- **Includes:** Monetary claims, consumer/trader claims, tenancy disputes, property recovery
- **Discovery/disclosure obligations apply**
- **Court-directed mediation** almost always attempted before trial

## Procedure Workflow: Lodgement → Hearing → Judgment

### Step 1: Lodge the Claim
1. Prepare CIV Form 3 (general) or CIV Form 4 (minor case) with statement of claim
2. Prepare supporting CIV Form 2 affidavit
3. Register for eCourts Portal (if not done — allow 24 hours for approval)
4. eLodge claim + affidavit + pay court fee
5. Receive on-screen confirmation of successful filing

### Step 2: Serve the Defendant
- Claimant must serve sealed copy on defendant
- Methods: personal service (preferred), post, email (if permitted), substituted service (if court allows)
- **Service must be at least 5 clear days before hearing**
- File Affidavit of Service with court

### Step 3: Defendant's Response
- Check the served claim form and current Magistrates Court rules before calculating the response deadline. **14 days from service** is common for service within WA; longer periods can apply for interstate or overseas service.
- Court seals defence form before defendant serves it on claimant

### Step 4: Case Management
**Minor case:** Automatic listing for status conference → court attempts settlement
**General procedure:** Registrar-led conciliation conference (confidential, without prejudice) → if unresolved, proceeds to trial

### Step 5: Trial and Judgment
- Oral evidence, affidavits, cross-examination before magistrate
- Judgment delivered with reasons
- Enforceable order issued

## Critical Deadlines — Civil

| Action | Timeframe | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Notice of intention to defend | Usually **14 days** from WA service | Verify against served form/rules; interstate/overseas service may differ |
| Statement of defence | Usually **14 days** from WA service | Via CIV Form 68; verify exact deadline |
| Service before hearing | **5 clear days** minimum | Before court date |
| Claim validity | **1 year** from lodgement | Must be served within this period |
| eCourts registration approval | **24 hours** | Allow for processing |
| Unlodged document expiry | **12 months** | Auto-deleted from eCourts |

## Mandatory Electronic Filing (eLodgment)

- **System:** eLodgment (Electronic Document Lodgment) — verify current portal requirements before filing
- **Mandatory from:** 23 September 2021 for all civil jurisdiction (except Restraining Orders Act 1997 applications)
- **Governed by:** Magistrates Court (General Rules) 2005 r12(1); Practice Direction 1 of 2021
- **Registration:** Free; takes up to 24 hours for approval
- **Helpdesk:** Courts Technology Group — **(08) 9425 2645** (8:15am–5pm Mon–Fri)
- **Email:** ctghelpdesk@justice.wa.gov.au
- **Portal:** www.ecourts.wa.gov.au
- **User guide:** [eCourts Portal User Guide (PDF)](https://www.magistratescourt.wa.gov.au/_files/eCourts_Portal_User_Guide.pdf)
- **FAQs:** [eLodgment FAQs](https://www.magistratescourt.wa.gov.au/E/elodgment_frequently_asked_questions.aspx)
- **Features:** Document lodgement, online fee payment, immediate sealed service copies to lodging party

## Mediation and Conciliation

- Court almost always attempts registrar-led conciliation before trial
- **Format:** Confidential meeting with trained court registrar
- **Without prejudice:** Statements cannot be used at trial if settlement fails
- Agreement can be incorporated into enforceable court order
- **Benefit:** Clarifies issues, reduces trial time and cost

## Remote and Audio Hearings

- **Practice Direction 2 of 2021** authorises audio appearances in civil proceedings
- Microsoft Teams used for remote hearings — [External Party Guide (PDF)](https://www.magistratescourt.wa.gov.au/_files/Court_Microsoft_Teams_External_Party_Guide.pdf)
- CCTV and remote witness rooms available at Perth and regional courts
- eTrials equipment available in CLC Perth courtrooms (exhibits entered into ICMS database)
- Parties may seek directions hearing for eTrial management
