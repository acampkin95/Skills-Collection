# FVRO Affidavit Drafting Templates (WA Magistrates Court)

> **WARNING**: These are templates for general guidance only. They must be reviewed by a WA lawyer and customised to the specific facts of each case before filing with the court. Do NOT file without legal advice.

## General Format Requirements

- **Court heading**: "IN THE MAGISTRATES COURT OF WESTERN AUSTRALIA AT [location]"
- **Case reference**: File number (if known)
- **Parties**: "Between [Applicant name] (Applicant/Protected Person) and [Respondent name] (Respondent/Person Bound)"
- **Affidavit title**: "AFFIDAVIT OF [FULL NAME]"
- **Opening**: "I, [full legal name], of [suburb, State], [occupation], state on oath / solemnly affirm:"
- **Numbered paragraphs**: Sequential, continuous (1, 2, 3...), one topic per paragraph
- **Exhibits**: Referenced as "Annexure A", "Annexure B" etc. — each exhibit signed by deponent and witness, marked with exhibit letter
- **Jurat**: "Sworn/Affirmed at [place] on [date] before me: [name, qualification of witness — JP, lawyer, registrar]"
- **Signatures**: Deponent and witness

## Template 1: Objection to Interim FVRO Becoming Final (s.31)

### Context

Respondent has been served with interim FVRO and has 21 days to object under s.31. This affidavit supports the objection and will be considered at conference or final hearing.

### Structure

```
AFFIDAVIT OF [FULL NAME]

I, [full legal name], of [suburb, State], [occupation], state on oath:

IDENTITY AND ROLE

1.  I am the respondent in these proceedings. I am the person bound by the
    interim family violence restraining order ("the interim FVRO") made on
    [date] by the [name] Magistrates Court, file number [number].

2.  The interim FVRO was served on me on [date]. I file this affidavit in
    support of my objection under section 31 of the Restraining Orders Act
    1997 (WA) within the 21-day objection period.

RESPONSE TO ALLEGATIONS

3.  The applicant alleges that on or about [date], I [summary of allegation].
    I deny this allegation. [Provide factual account of what actually occurred,
    in respondent's version].

4.  [Repeat for each material allegation — one paragraph per allegation.
    Either deny outright ("I deny that I [conduct]. This did not happen.")
    or provide context ("On the date alleged, I did [X], but this was not
    family violence because [explanation].").]

5.  [If there is counter-evidence — messages, witness statements — reference
    them here. E.g., "Attached at Annexure A is a text message from the
    applicant sent to me on [date] which shows [context]."]

FACTUAL ACCOUNT

6.  [Provide respondent's chronological narrative of the relevant events,
    addressing the incidents alleged by the applicant with respondent's version.]

7.  [Include any context about the relationship, circumstances, or
    respondent's state of mind that assists the court.]

CONDITIONS AND HARDSHIP (IF APPLICABLE)

8.  [If agreeing to some form of order but seeking variation of conditions:]
    I do not oppose an order being made, but I respectfully submit that the
    following conditions cause me unnecessary hardship: [identify specific
    condition and explain hardship].

PROPOSED CONDITIONS / UNDERTAKINGS

9.  I am willing to consent to an order containing the following conditions:
    [list proposed conditions, e.g.:]
    (a) That I refrain from any contact with the applicant;
    (b) That I not approach or attend within [distance] of the applicant's
        residence at [address];
    (c) [Other conditions respondent accepts].

10. I further undertake to [specific commitments, e.g.:]
    (a) Engage in anger management / substance abuse counselling;
    (b) Continue attending [treatment/program];
    (c) [Other undertakings].

11. These proposed conditions and undertakings would provide the applicant
    with protection while allowing me to maintain my [employment / parenting
    relationship / housing / medical access].

[Sworn/Affirmed at [place] on [date] before me.]
```

## Template 2: Application to Vary Interim FVRO (Hardship — s.45, s.46(4)(a)(iii))

### Context

Respondent applies to vary conditions of interim FVRO, claiming serious and unnecessary hardship under s.46(4)(a)(iii). The leave hearing is ex parte (s.46(2)).

### Structure

```
AFFIDAVIT OF [FULL NAME]
IN SUPPORT OF APPLICATION TO VARY INTERIM FVRO

I, [full legal name], of [suburb, State], [occupation], state on oath:

ORDER IDENTIFICATION

1.  I am the person bound by the interim family violence restraining order
    ("the interim FVRO") made on [date] by the [name] Magistrates Court,
    file number [number].

2.  I apply to vary the interim FVRO pursuant to section 45(1)(c) of the
    Restraining Orders Act 1997 (WA). I seek leave of the court under
    section 46(4)(a)(iii) on the ground that the restraints imposed are
    causing me serious and unnecessary hardship.

SPECIFIC CONDITION CAUSING HARDSHIP

3.  The condition I seek to vary is Condition [number], which provides:
    "[quote the condition precisely, e.g., 'The respondent must not come
    within 500 metres of [address]']."

HARDSHIP — EMPLOYMENT

4.  I am employed as [job title] at [employer name], located at [address].
    My workplace falls within the [distance] exclusion zone imposed by
    Condition [number].

5.  I am unable to attend my workplace without breaching the interim FVRO.
    My employer has informed me (letter attached, Annexure A) that no
    alternative work location is available and that I face termination
    unless I can attend the workplace.

6.  I have been employed at [employer] for [duration]. My income is $[amount]
    per [period]. I have no other source of income. Loss of employment would
    cause me severe financial hardship.

HARDSHIP — HOUSING [if applicable]

7.  [The exclusion zone includes the residence at [address] where I currently
    reside / have resided for [duration]. I have no alternative accommodation.
    I cannot afford to relocate. I request variation to allow me to remain
    at my current address / access my belongings.]

HARDSHIP — MEDICAL [if applicable]

8.  [I have a medical condition requiring treatment at [hospital/clinic] at
    [address]. This facility is within the exclusion zone. My condition is
    [describe]. My treating doctor has advised (letter attached, Annexure B)
    that treatment cannot be deferred. No alternative facility is available
    within reasonable distance.]

HARDSHIP — PARENTING [if applicable]

9.  [The applicant and I have [number] child(ren), aged [ages]. I share
    parenting [arrangement]. The exclusion zone includes the applicant's
    address where the child(ren) reside during [times]. I cannot exercise
    my parenting time without risking breach. I request variation to allow
    contact with my child(ren) at [neutral location / supervised contact
    centre].]

"SERIOUS AND UNNECESSARY" THRESHOLD

10. The hardship described above is serious because [it threatens my
    employment / housing / health / relationship with my child(ren) /
    financial stability].

11. The condition is unnecessary for the applicant's protection because
    [explanation — e.g., applicant has moved to a different address;
    I have complied with the no-contact condition; there is no ongoing
    risk of contact at my workplace].

URGENCY

12. This application is urgent because [specific time-sensitive element,
    e.g., "My employer has given me until [date] to return to work or face
    termination" / "My medical appointment is scheduled for [date]" /
    "I face eviction on [date]"].

PROPOSED VARIED CONDITION

13. I propose the condition be varied to:
    "[Draft alternative condition, e.g., 'The respondent may attend
    [workplace address] for the purposes of employment only, on [days/times],
    proceeding directly to and from the workplace. The respondent shall not
    contact or approach the applicant at any time.']"

    Alternatively: "I request the exclusion zone be reduced from [current
    distance] to [proposed distance], which would allow me to [access
    workplace / residence] while maintaining separation from the applicant."

SAFETY REASSURANCE

14. The variation will not compromise the applicant's safety because:
    (a) I have not initiated contact with the applicant since the interim
        FVRO was made;
    (b) I accept the no-contact condition absolutely;
    (c) [There have been no further incidents since the interim order];
    (d) [Any other safety factors].

15. I undertake to [specific commitments that address safety concerns].

[Sworn/Affirmed at [place] on [date] before me.]

ANNEXURES:
- Annexure A: [Employer letter]
- Annexure B: [Medical letter, if applicable]
- Annexure C: [Other supporting documents]
```

## Template 3: Application to Cancel Final FVRO (Substantial Change — s.45, s.46(4)(a)(ii))

### Context

Respondent applies to cancel a final FVRO, claiming substantial change in circumstances under s.46(4)(a)(ii). Leave hearing is ex parte.

### Structure

```
AFFIDAVIT OF [FULL NAME]
IN SUPPORT OF APPLICATION TO CANCEL FINAL FVRO

I, [full legal name], of [suburb, State], [occupation], state on oath:

ORDER IDENTIFICATION AND DURATION

1.  I am the person bound by the final family violence restraining order
    ("the FVRO") made on [date] by the [name] Magistrates Court, file
    number [number]. The FVRO has been in force for [duration]. It is
    due to expire on [date] / is of indefinite duration.

2.  I apply to cancel the FVRO pursuant to section 45(1)(c) of the
    Restraining Orders Act 1997 (WA). I seek leave of the court under
    section 46(4)(a)(ii) on the ground that there has been a substantial
    change in the relevant circumstances since the order was made.

ORIGINAL CIRCUMSTANCES (BRIEF)

3.  The FVRO was made in circumstances where the court found [brief neutral
    summary of what led to the order — without accepting findings as true
    if disputing, or acknowledging if accepting]. At that time, [context
    about respondent's situation, e.g., mental health, substance use,
    relationship dynamics].

SUBSTANTIAL CHANGES SINCE THE ORDER

Sub-section A: No contact / passage of time

4.  Since the final FVRO was made on [date], I have not had any contact
    with the applicant. This is now a period of [years/months]. There
    have been no further incidents, complaints, or concerns raised by
    the applicant or police.

5.  [If applicable:] The applicant has not sought to extend or vary the
    order, nor has she/he raised concerns with police. This suggests the
    risk assessed at the time of the order has abated.

Sub-section B: Successful rehabilitation / treatment

6.  I have completed the following programs since the order was made:
    (a) [Behaviour change program — name, provider, dates, certificate
        attached at Annexure A];
    (b) [Anger management course — name, provider, dates, certificate
        attached at Annexure B];
    (c) [Substance abuse treatment — name, provider, dates, completion
        letter attached at Annexure C].

7.  I attend ongoing [counselling / psychology / psychiatry] with [provider
    name] at [frequency]. My treating [professional] has assessed that I
    have made significant progress and am at low risk of re-offending.
    Their letter is attached at Annexure D.

8.  I have been compliant with all conditions of the FVRO throughout its
    duration. I have [not been charged with any offence / maintained good
    behaviour / engaged proactively with services].

Sub-section C: Changed life circumstances

9.  I have relocated to [location], which is [distance] from the applicant's
    current residence. Our previous shared circumstances [no longer exist /
    have materially changed].

10. [If applicable:] I am now in a [new relationship / different living
    arrangement / new employment] that does not involve the applicant. The
    family violence risk was specific to our [past relationship / cohabitation /
    substance use at the time]. That context no longer exists.

11. [If applicable:] The applicant has [remarried / relocated interstate /
    changed circumstances]. Contact between us is unlikely and unnecessary.

Sub-section D: Legal/formal changes [if applicable]

12. [A parenting order is now in place specifying contact terms. Communication
    is limited to parenting matters, routed through [intermediary / app /
    supervised]. See Annexure E.]

13. [The underlying criminal matter has been resolved. Charges were
    withdrawn / acquitted / dealt with. See Annexure F.]

STATUTORY TEST: SUBSTANTIAL CHANGE

14. Each of the circumstances described above represents a material change
    since the FVRO was made on [date]. Cumulatively, they demonstrate that
    the risk of family violence which the order was designed to address has
    substantially diminished.

NO ONGOING RISK

15. The original risk of family violence no longer exists or is now minimal
    because:
    (a) [duration] of demonstrated compliance with no incidents;
    (b) Successful completion of [programs/treatment];
    (c) Material change in circumstances [relocation / new arrangements];
    (d) [Psychological/psychiatric evidence of rehabilitation — Annexure D].

PROTECTED PERSON SAFETY

16. Cancellation will not place the applicant at risk. I have demonstrated
    sustained compliance with the order. I have completed treatment and
    rehabilitation. Circumstances have changed materially. The order is
    no longer necessary for the applicant's protection.

17. [Optional undertaking:] I am willing to undertake to:
    (a) Continue in [treatment / counselling];
    (b) Maintain no contact with the applicant;
    (c) [Other conditions respondent offers voluntarily].

PROPORTIONALITY

18. [If order is indefinite:] An indefinite FVRO is a serious restraint on
    my liberty. After [duration] of demonstrated compliance, rehabilitation,
    and material change in circumstances, continued indefinite restraint is
    disproportionate.

[Sworn/Affirmed at [place] on [date] before me.]

ANNEXURES:
- Annexure A: [Behaviour change program certificate]
- Annexure B: [Anger management certificate]
- Annexure C: [Treatment completion letter]
- Annexure D: [Treating professional's letter / psychiatric assessment]
- Annexure E: [Parenting order, if applicable]
- Annexure F: [Criminal matter resolution, if applicable]
```

## Template 4: Breach Encouragement Evidence (s.46(4)(a)(i) Leave Ground)

### Context

Respondent applies to vary/cancel FVRO and relies on evidence that the protected person persistently invited or encouraged breach under s.46(4)(a)(i). This affidavit supports the leave application.

### Structure

```
AFFIDAVIT OF [FULL NAME]
IN SUPPORT OF APPLICATION TO [VARY/CANCEL] FVRO
(BREACH ENCOURAGEMENT EVIDENCE — s.46(4)(a)(i))

I, [full legal name], of [suburb, State], [occupation], state on oath:

THRESHOLD STATEMENT

1.  I am the person bound by the [interim/final] FVRO made on [date],
    file number [number].

2.  I apply to [vary/cancel] the FVRO under section 45(1)(c) of the
    Restraining Orders Act 1997 (WA). I seek leave under section
    46(4)(a)(i) on the ground that the person protected by the order
    has persistently invited or encouraged me to breach the order, or
    by her/his actions has persistently attempted to cause me to breach
    the order.

3.  [If also relying on other grounds:] I also rely on section
    46(4)(a)(ii) (substantial change in circumstances) and/or section
    46(4)(a)(iii) (serious and unnecessary hardship).

CHRONOLOGICAL INCIDENT NARRATIVE

4.  On or about [date — earliest incident], approximately [timeframe]
    after the [interim/final] FVRO was made, [protected person's name]
    contacted me via [method: text message / phone call / email / social
    media / in person]. [She/He] said [quote or describe exactly what was
    said or done]. A screenshot of this [message/communication] is attached
    at Annexure A.

5.  This amounted to an invitation to breach the no-contact condition of
    the FVRO because [explain how this encouraged breach — e.g., "it
    invited me to respond to her/his message" / "it asked me to attend
    her/his residence"].

6.  On or about [date — second incident], approximately [timeframe] after
    the first incident, [protected person] again contacted me via [method]
    and said [quote]. This was the second occasion on which [she/he]
    encouraged me to breach the order. A screenshot is at Annexure B.

7.  [Continue for each documented incident — one or two paragraphs per
    incident, with date, method, content, and exhibit reference. Aim for
    3-5+ incidents to demonstrate "persistence".]

8.  [If witness evidence available:] On [date], [protected person] told
    our mutual [friend/family member] [witness name], in my presence,
    "[quote, e.g., 'I've been encouraging [respondent] to contact me
    because I don't actually want the order']." [Witness name] has
    provided a statement (Annexure [letter]).

PATTERN STATEMENT

9.  Over the period [first date] to [last date / present], [protected
    person] has on at least [number] occasions invited, encouraged, or
    attempted to cause me to breach the FVRO. This is a clear, persistent
    pattern — not an isolated incident. Each time, [she/he] initiated
    contact and/or encouraged me to respond or meet.

RESPONDENT'S RESISTANCE

10. I have [attempted to resist / successfully resisted] these invitations.
    [Choose applicable:]

    (a) [If respondent did NOT breach:] Despite persistent encouragement,
        I have not responded to [protected person's] communications. I have
        maintained strict compliance with the FVRO throughout. This
        demonstrates my commitment to obeying the order.

    (b) [If respondent DID breach on some occasions:] I attempted to resist
        these invitations. I [did not respond to most / declined to meet /
        told her/him the order was in force]. However, on [number] occasions
        I was unable to resist and responded, which I acknowledge may have
        constituted breach. I take responsibility for those occasions whilst
        noting the persistent encouragement that preceded them.

SIGNIFICANCE FOR VARIATION/CANCELLATION

11. This pattern of encouragement demonstrates that:
    (a) The protected person does not genuinely fear me or require the
        protection the FVRO was designed to provide;
    (b) The FVRO is not serving its intended protective purpose — the very
        person it is designed to protect is actively undermining it;
    (c) The persistent encouragement places me at ongoing risk of criminal
        prosecution for conduct initiated by the protected person; and
    (d) [Variation/cancellation] would not place the protected person at
        risk, as [she/he] continues to actively seek my contact.

12. I am aware that under section 61B of the Act, the protected person's
    encouragement is not a defence to a breach charge. I am not seeking
    to excuse any breach. I am applying through the proper court process
    under sections 45-46 for [variation/cancellation] of the order.

[Sworn/Affirmed at [place] on [date] before me.]

ANNEXURES:
- Annexure A: [Screenshot of first communication with timestamp]
- Annexure B: [Screenshot of second communication with timestamp]
- Annexure C: [Additional communications / call logs]
- Annexure D: [Witness statement, if available]
- Annexure E: [Any other supporting evidence]
```

## Template 5: Mental Health Treatment Conflict Variation (s.46(4)(a)(iii))

### Context

Respondent is subject to both FVRO and involuntary MHA treatment order. Treatment clinic/hospital is within FVRO exclusion zone. Respondent applies to vary on hardship ground.

### Structure

```
AFFIDAVIT OF [FULL NAME]
IN SUPPORT OF APPLICATION TO VARY FVRO
(MENTAL HEALTH TREATMENT CONFLICT)

I, [full legal name], of [suburb, State], [occupation], state on oath:

FVRO CONDITION IDENTIFICATION

1.  I am the person bound by the [interim/final] FVRO made on [date],
    file number [number].

2.  I apply to vary the FVRO under section 45(1)(c). I seek leave under
    section 46(4)(a)(iii) on the ground of serious and unnecessary
    hardship.

3.  The condition I seek to vary is Condition [number], which provides:
    "[quote precisely, e.g., 'The respondent must not approach or attend
    within 500 metres of [address]']."

MENTAL HEALTH STATUS

4.  I am currently subject to an involuntary [community patient order /
    inpatient treatment order] under the Mental Health Act 2014 (WA),
    made on [date] by [psychiatrist / Mental Health Tribunal]. A copy of
    the order is attached at Annexure A.

5.  I have been diagnosed with [diagnosis, e.g., schizophrenia / bipolar
    disorder / major depressive disorder with psychotic features].

6.  Under my treatment order, I am required to attend [clinic/hospital
    name], located at [address], [frequency, e.g., twice weekly / weekly].
    My treating psychiatrist is [name].

CONFLICT

7.  The address of my mandated treatment [clinic/hospital] is [address].
    This falls within the [distance] exclusion zone imposed by Condition
    [number] of the FVRO.

8.  If I attend my mandated mental health treatment, I am in breach of
    the FVRO. Conversely, if I comply with the FVRO exclusion zone, I
    cannot access my mandated mental health treatment. I am in an
    impossible position between two legal obligations.

HARDSHIP

9.  This creates serious and unnecessary hardship:
    (a) My mental health condition is serious and requires ongoing
        treatment. Non-attendance at clinic places me at risk of relapse,
        hospitalisation, and deterioration.
    (b) My treating psychiatrist, [name], has advised (letter attached,
        Annexure B) that non-attendance will exacerbate my symptoms and
        pose a risk to my health.
    (c) There is no alternative treatment clinic available to me — this
        is the only [public mental health service / specialist clinic] in
        the region providing [type of treatment].
    (d) Non-compliance with the Mental Health Act treatment order may also
        result in administrative consequences including an order to attend
        (s.128 MHA), transport order (s.129 MHA), or inpatient detention
        (s.131 MHA).

URGENCY

10. My next mandated appointment is [date]. I must seek urgent variation,
    as I will be in breach of the FVRO if I attend, or in breach of my
    Mental Health Act treatment order if I do not.

PROPOSED VARIATION

11. I request that Condition [number] be varied to:
    "The respondent may attend [clinic/hospital name] at [address] for
    the purposes of mental health treatment only, on [specified days and
    times, e.g., Tuesdays and Fridays, 2:00–3:00 pm], for the purpose
    of attending appointments with [treating psychiatrist / clinic name].
    The respondent shall proceed directly to the clinic and depart directly
    after the appointment. The respondent shall not remain in the area
    outside appointment times. The respondent shall not approach or
    contact [protected person's name] at any time."

SAFETY REASSURANCE

12. The variation will not compromise the applicant's safety because:
    (a) I accept the no-contact condition absolutely;
    (b) The clinic is a professional health setting — there is no
        opportunity for family violence;
    (c) I have not initiated contact with the applicant since the FVRO
        was made;
    (d) My treating psychiatrist can provide supervision / confirmation
        of my attendance.

13. I am willing to have [the clinic / my treating psychiatrist] confirm
    my attendance at each appointment if the court requires it.

[Sworn/Affirmed at [place] on [date] before me.]

ANNEXURES:
- Annexure A: [Copy of Mental Health Act treatment order]
- Annexure B: [Letter from treating psychiatrist confirming diagnosis,
  treatment plan, clinic location, risk of non-attendance]
- Annexure C: [Clinic contact details / map showing location within
  exclusion zone, if helpful]
```

## Drafting Notes for All Templates

### Tone & Style
- Neutral and factual — avoid emotional language, accusations, or editorialising
- Compelling through facts and evidence, not argument
- State what happened; let the evidence speak
- Where expressing belief rather than personal knowledge, say "I believe, based on [basis]"

### Evidence Quality
- **Best**: Original documents (court orders, letters, certificates)
- **Good**: Screenshots with visible timestamps and metadata
- **Acceptable**: Contemporaneous notes (handwritten, dated at time of event)
- **Weakest**: Reconstructed notes written long after events — explain delay

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Admitting facts that establish family violence (unless strategy is to consent to order)
- Argumentative or hostile tone — court will disregard
- Unsupported assertions — everything claimed must have an exhibit or explanation
- Mentioning irrelevant grievances (property disputes, custody battles unless directly relevant)
- Failing to address safety — court's primary concern is always protected person's safety
