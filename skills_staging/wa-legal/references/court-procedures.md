## 1. Magistrates Court — Civil Matters

**Jurisdiction**: Civil claims up to $75,000
**Website**: magistratescourt.wa.gov.au
**Minor Cases Division (Small Claims)**: Claims up to $10,000 — simplified procedures, no lawyers without leave

### How to Start a Civil Claim

1. Complete the appropriate form:
   - **Claim** (General): *Form 1 — Civil Claim* (claims over $10,000 up to $75,000)
   - **Small Claims**: *Minor Cases Division — Small Claim form* (up to $10,000)
2. Pay the filing fee (see Section 10)
3. Lodge at any Magistrates Court registry (or eCourts Portal for eligible matters)
4. Court issues the claim and assigns a file number
5. Serve the claim on the defendant (see Section 9)
6. Defendant has **21 days** to file a notice of defence (or 28 days if service outside WA)

### Typical Civil Procedure

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Filing** | Claim lodged with court; defendant served |
| **Response** | Defendant files notice of intention to defend (or defence) |
| **Directions hearing / Mention** | Court sets timetable for evidence and hearing |
| **Pre-trial conference** | Compulsory settlement conference |
| **Trial / Final hearing** | Evidence and submissions; judgment |
| **Enforcement** | Judgment creditor enforces via garnishee, examination, or warrant |

### Key Forms (Civil)

| Form | Title | Use |
|------|-------|-----|
| Form 1 | Civil Claim | Commence claim $10k–$75k |
| Form 2 | Notice of Intention to Defend | Defendant enters a defence |
| Form 3 | Default Judgment | Where defendant fails to respond |
| Form 6 | Garnishee Order | Enforcement against wages/bank accounts |
| Form 8 | Examination Notice | Compel debtor to disclose assets |

> Download all forms from magistratescourt.wa.gov.au → *Forms*

## 2. Magistrates Court — Criminal Matters

### Summary Offences

Most criminal matters in the Magistrates Court proceed by **complaint** (police-initiated) or **prosecution notice**. Defendants generally do not file documents to start the process — they receive a court summons or are charged at a police station.

### What Defendants Need to Do

1. **Attend court** on the date shown on the summons or court notice
2. **Apply for duty lawyer** assistance on arrival (Legal Aid WA — arrive 30–45 min early)
3. **Enter a plea**: Guilty or Not Guilty at the first mention
4. If **Not Guilty**: matter is set down for hearing; disclosure of evidence follows
5. If **Guilty**: sentenced at the mention or sentencing hearing

### Bail Applications

- **Police bail**: Police can grant bail at charge; conditions set by police
- **Court bail**: If bail refused by police, apply at the next court appearance
- **Bail review**: Apply to the District Court if refused bail by Magistrate

### Spent Convictions

After a qualifying period, some convictions become "spent" and need not be disclosed:
- **Summary offences**: 5 years after the order was made (10 years for juveniles)
- **Indictable offences**: 10 years after the order was made
- Spent convictions cannot be disclosed in many contexts (job applications, etc.)
- See *Spent Convictions Act 1988* (WA)

## 3. Magistrates Court — Restraining Orders

> **For detailed FVRO/VRO procedures, see the `wa-fvro` skill and its reference files.**

### Key Points

- **Initial FVRO application**: Check current Magistrates Court WA guidance for the correct filing channel before advising whether registry attendance or an online pathway is available.
- **Respondent objection** (s.31): Complete the endorsed section of the served copy and return to the registrar within 21 days of service
- **Variation/Cancellation** (s.45 application): Use **Form 12** — *Restraining Order — Application to Vary or Cancel*; lodge in person or via eCourts Portal (if existing file)
- **No filing fee** for FVRO applications or variation/cancellation

## 4. District Court

**Jurisdiction**:
- Civil: $75,000–$750,000
- Criminal: Indictable offences (except murder, treason, wilful murder); criminal appeals from Magistrates Court

**Website**: districtcourt.wa.gov.au

### Starting a Civil Proceeding

1. Prepare a **Writ of Summons** (or originating summons for non-disputed matters)
2. File at the District Court registry + pay filing fee
3. Serve on the defendant per service rules
4. Defendant files Notice of Appearance within **10 days** of service (within WA)

### District Court Criminal Appeals (from Magistrates Court)

- File a **Notice of Appeal** within **21 days** of the order appealed against
- Obtain the Magistrates Court record (transcripts, exhibits)
- Appeal is heard de novo (rehearing) for sentence; on the record for conviction appeals
- Contact districtcourt.wa.gov.au for current forms

### Key District Court Registry

**Perth District Court**: 500 Hay Street, Perth WA 6000 | (08) 9425 2222

## 5. Supreme Court

**Jurisdiction**:
- Civil: Unlimited (typically used for claims over $750,000 or complex legal questions)
- Criminal: Most serious indictable offences (murder, treason, wilful murder); appeals from District Court
- Appeals: From District Court on questions of law; judicial review

**Website**: supremecourt.wa.gov.au

### Commencing Proceedings

- Civil: Writ of Summons, Originating Motion, or Originating Summons (depending on matter type)
- Appeal: Notice of Appeal filed within time limit (typically 21 days of order)
- Judicial review: Originating motion; strict time limits apply (often 6 months from the decision)

**Perth Supreme Court**: Stirling Gardens, 497 Hay Street, Perth WA 6000 | (08) 9421 5333

### Court of Appeal

Hears appeals from the Supreme Court and District Court on questions of law. By leave in most civil matters; as of right in some criminal matters.

## 6. Family Court of WA

**Jurisdiction**: WA is the only state with its own Family Court under state legislation (*Family Court Act 1997*). Handles divorce, parenting orders, property settlements, and child support matters.

**Website**: familycourt.wa.gov.au
**Registry**: Level 7, 111 St Georges Terrace, Perth WA 6000 | (08) 9224 8222
**National enquiry line**: 1300 352 000

### Key Family Law Matters

| Matter | Form | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| Divorce | Application for Divorce | 12-month separation required |
| Parenting orders | Application for Orders | Can also be done by consent |
| Property settlement | Application for Orders | Time limit: 12 months after divorce is final |
| Interim orders | Application for Interim Orders | Urgent matters; heard quickly |

### Family Court Self-Help

- Duty lawyer available at the Family Court registry
- Federal Circuit and Family Court of Australia website (fcfcoa.gov.au) has helpful guides
- Family Relationships Online: familyrelationships.gov.au — mediation and relationship counselling

## 7. State Administrative Tribunal (SAT)

**Jurisdiction**: Review of administrative decisions; guardianship and administration; disciplinary matters; residential tenancies (limited); equal opportunity.

**Website**: sat.justice.wa.gov.au
**Registry**: 12 St Georges Terrace, Perth WA 6000 | (08) 9219 3111

### Commencing a Review

1. Identify the reviewable decision and applicable legislation
2. Check the time limit for filing (commonly 28 days from decision; varies by Act)
3. Complete the relevant application form from sat.justice.wa.gov.au
4. File at the SAT registry or by email (some matters); no filing fee for many applications
5. SAT will notify you of the hearing date

### Key SAT Divisions

| Division | Typical Matters |
|----------|----------------|
| **Review and Regulatory** | Government decision review, professional discipline, planning |
| **Guardianship** | Guardianship/administration orders for people with impaired decision-making capacity |
| **Equal Opportunity** | Discrimination complaints referred from EOC WA |
| **Commercial and Civil** | Strata disputes, retirement village disputes, retail tenancy |

## 8. eCourts Portal — Online Filing

**URL**: ecourts.justice.wa.gov.au

### What You Can Do Online

- View your existing matter file (with a matter file number)
- Lodge documents in **existing FVRO/VRO matters** (eLodgment)
- Lodge documents in **existing Magistrates Court civil matters**
- Pay filing fees by credit/debit card
- View court dates and listings

### What Requires In-Person Filing

- New initial FVRO/VRO applications, unless current court guidance provides an online pathway
- First filing in any matter where no eCourts file exists
- Affidavits and forms in matters without an eCourts Portal file number

### How to Use eCourts Portal

1. Go to ecourts.justice.wa.gov.au
2. Click **Register** and create an account (free)
3. Once registered, link to your existing matter using the file/case number from court documents
4. Navigate to **eLodgment** → locate matter by file number
5. Upload documents in PDF format
6. Pay any applicable fee by card
7. Receive a lodgment confirmation email

**Technical help**: (08) 9425 2222 or see magistratescourt.wa.gov.au

## 9. Service of Documents

### Personal Service

The default method for most court documents. The document is handed directly to the person. Personal service is required for:
- Originating documents commencing proceedings (Writs, Claims)
- Subpoenas
- Many enforcement documents

**How to personally serve**: Hand the document to the person and (if required) state what it is. Note the date, time, location, and appearance of the person served.

**Who can serve**: Generally anyone over 18 who is not a party to the proceedings. Process servers are available for hire.

### Postal Service

For documents where personal service is not required (e.g., later correspondence in proceedings):
- Ordinary post: deemed served 7 days after posting
- Registered post: evidence of delivery; deemed served on the date of delivery or 7 days after posting (whichever is earlier)

### Substituted Service

Where personal service is impractical, a court can order substituted service (e.g., service by email, newspaper advertisement, service on a family member). An application must be made to the court.

### Service on Corporations

- Companies registered under the *Corporations Act 2001*: Leave documents at the registered office, or serve by post to the registered office
- WA statutory bodies: Check the specific Act for service provisions

### Service Outside WA

- Additional time to respond (e.g., 28 days instead of 21 for civil claims)
- Special rules apply for service in other states (interstate service agreement) and overseas (Hague Service Convention for some countries)

### Affidavit of Service

After serving documents, prepare an **Affidavit of Service** (sworn statement of the facts of service). This is filed with the court as proof of service. Include:
- Full name and address of the person serving
- Date, time, and place of service
- How documents were identified to the person served
- Any distinguishing features of the person served

## 10. Filing Fees & Fee Waivers

### Magistrates Court (WA) — Fee Check

> Always verify at magistratescourt.wa.gov.au before filing — fees are updated periodically.

Do not hard-code fees into court-facing documents. Retrieve the current fee PDF/page immediately before filing, then record:

| Matter | Verification required |
|--------|-----------------------|
| FVRO / VRO application | Check current Magistrates Court fees/restraining orders guidance |
| FVRO variation / cancellation (Form 12) | Check current Magistrates Court fees/restraining orders guidance |
| Civil claim filing | Check amount claimed and current fee schedule |
| Notice of defence / response document | Check current fee schedule |
| Subpoena / witness summons | Check current fee schedule |
| Enforcement application | Check current fee schedule |
| Criminal matter document | Check whether any fee applies |

### District Court (WA) — Approximate Fees

| Matter | Fee |
|--------|-----|
| Civil Writ (claim $75,001–$250,000) | ~$660 |
| Civil Writ (claim over $250,000) | ~$1,100 |
| Criminal appeal from Magistrates Court | ~$220 |

### Supreme Court (WA) — Approximate Fees

| Matter | Fee |
|--------|-----|
| Writ of summons / originating process | ~$1,800–$2,700 |
| Criminal appeal (indictable) | ~$220 |

### SAT Fees

- Many SAT applications are free (guardianship, equal opportunity, planning)
- Commercial/civil SAT matters may attract fees — check sat.justice.wa.gov.au

### Fee Waivers

A fee waiver is available where payment would cause **financial hardship**:

1. Request a **Fee Waiver Application** form at the court registry
2. Provide evidence of income and assets (Centrelink statement, bank statements, pay slips)
3. Submit the fee waiver application at the same time as filing
4. Registry assesses and either grants or refuses the waiver

**Likely to be granted**: Centrelink recipients, low-income earners, people experiencing financial hardship
**Not guaranteed**: The registry has discretion; complex or high-value matters may require more evidence

## 12. Appeals

### Appeals from Magistrates Court

| Type | Appeal To | Time Limit | Grounds |
|------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Criminal conviction (summary) | District Court | **21 days** from order | Questions of law or fact; rehearing |
| Criminal sentence (summary) | District Court | **21 days** from order | Severity of sentence |
| Civil judgment | District Court | **21 days** from order | Error of law or fact |
| FVRO (final order) | District Court | **21 days** from order | Error of law or fact |

### Appeals from District Court

| Type | Appeal To | Time Limit | Grounds |
|------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Criminal conviction (indictable) | Supreme Court (Court of Appeal) | **21 days** from order | Questions of law; with leave for questions of fact |
| Criminal sentence | Court of Appeal | **21 days** | Manifest excess or error of law |
| Civil judgment | Court of Appeal | **21 days** | Error of law; leave required for questions of fact |

### Judicial Review (Supreme Court)

- Used to challenge the **lawfulness** of a government or tribunal decision
- Grounds: jurisdictional error, denial of natural justice, failure to consider relevant matters, taking into account irrelevant matters
- **Time limit**: Generally 6 months from the decision (can be strict — act promptly)
- Commence by Originating Motion in the Supreme Court

### General Appeal Principles

- Time limits are strict — missing the limit usually requires leave of the appeal court to proceed out of time
- An appeal does NOT automatically stay (suspend) the original order — apply for a stay if needed
- New evidence is generally not admissible on appeal (some exceptions for criminal matters)
- Seek legal advice before commencing an appeal — costs can be awarded against unsuccessful appellants

## Checklist: Filing Documents at Magistrates Court (General)

- [ ] Correct form downloaded from magistratescourt.wa.gov.au (or collected from registry)
- [ ] Form completed in full; no blank fields left
- [ ] Supporting affidavit prepared and signed/sworn before JP, lawyer, or court registrar (if required)
- [ ] Annexures numbered and signed (labelled Annexure A, B, C…)
- [ ] Copies made: one for court file + one for each other party + one for your records
- [ ] Filing fee determined and fee waiver form prepared (if required)
- [ ] Original + copies taken to registry during business hours
- [ ] Affidavit signed and sworn/affirmed before filing (not after)
- [ ] File number recorded when issued by registry
