# Brand Voice Framework

## Brand Architecture Model

### Layer 1: Brand Purpose
The fundamental reason the brand exists beyond profit.

**Purpose Statement Template:**
```
We exist to [action] for [audience] by [method] because [belief].
```

**Example:**
"We exist to democratise great design for emerging creators by making professional tools accessible because creativity shouldn't be gatekept by budget."

---

### Layer 2: Brand Positioning

**Positioning Statement Template:**
```
For [target audience] who [need/want],
[Brand] is the [category] that [key benefit]
because [reason to believe].
```

**Competitive Positioning Frameworks:**

**Frame of Reference:** What category do you compete in?
**Point of Difference:** What makes you distinctly better?
**Point of Parity:** Where must you match competitors?

---

### Layer 3: Brand Personality

Define brand as a human with consistent traits across all touchpoints.

**Personality Spectrum Framework:**
```
Dimension 1: Serious ←——→ Playful
Dimension 2: Traditional ←——→ Progressive
Dimension 3: Exclusive ←——→ Accessible
Dimension 4: Reserved ←——→ Bold
```

Plot your brand on each dimension. Consistency matters more than extremes.

**Personality Traits (Choose 3-4):**
- Curious, Bold, Warm, Sharp, Grounded, Rebellious, Nurturing, Witty, Earnest, Refined, Raw, Optimistic, Candid, Imaginative

---

### Layer 4: Brand Voice

**Voice Attributes Template:**
Define each attribute with a spectrum and examples.

```
Attribute: [Name]
Description: [What it means]
Spectrum: [Less] ←——→ [More]
Do: [Example of doing it well]
Don't: [Example of doing it poorly]
```

**Example Voice Attributes:**

**Conversational**
We speak like a knowledgeable friend, not a textbook.
- Do: "Here's the thing about sustainable packaging..."
- Don't: "Pursuant to environmental considerations..."

**Confident**
We know our stuff and say it directly.
- Do: "This works. Here's why."
- Don't: "We think maybe possibly this could potentially work..."

**Inclusive**
We speak to everyone without exclusion.
- Do: "Whether you're a first-timer or a pro..."
- Don't: "For the discerning connoisseur..."

---

### Layer 5: Brand Tone

Tone flexes based on context while voice stays consistent.

**Tone Matrix:**

| Context | Tone Shift | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Celebrating wins | Enthusiastic, warm | "You absolute legend!" |
| Addressing problems | Calm, solution-focused | "We hear you. Here's what we're doing." |
| Educational content | Clear, encouraging | "Let's break this down step by step." |
| Sales moments | Confident, benefit-led | "Ready to level up? Let's go." |
| Crisis communication | Direct, empathetic | "We got this wrong. Here's how we're fixing it." |

## Messaging Hierarchy

### Primary Message
Single sentence capturing core value proposition. Used in hero moments.

### Secondary Messages
3-5 supporting messages that expand on primary. Used in body content.

### Proof Points
Facts, stats, testimonials that substantiate messages. Used to build credibility.

**Message Development Template:**
```
Message: [Claim]
So what?: [Why audience should care]
Proof: [Evidence]
```

---

## Voice Application by Channel

### Social Media
- Shorter sentences, punchier rhythm
- Emoji use aligned with audience expectations
- Platform-native language (varies by platform)
- Personality shines strongest here

### Website
- Clear hierarchy, scannability
- Benefit-led headlines
- Progressive disclosure of detail
- CTAs that inspire action

### Email
- Subject lines that earn opens
- Personal but not presumptuous
- Respect inbox as intimate space
- Clear single action per email

### Long-form Content
- Narrative structure
- Show expertise without condescension
- Visual breaks and formatting
- Generous with value, strategic with CTAs
