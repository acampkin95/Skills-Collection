## 1. Legal Aid WA

**Main line**: 1300 650 579
**Website**: legalaid.wa.gov.au
**Hours**: Monday–Friday 8:30am–5:00pm AWST

### What Legal Aid Provides

- **Duty lawyer service**: Free on-the-day legal assistance at Magistrates Courts, District Courts, Supreme Court, Family Court, Children's Court, and Mental Health Tribunal
- **Grants of legal aid**: Funded representation for eligible clients
- **Legal advice**: Phone and in-person advice appointments
- **Self-help materials**: Guides, fact sheets, and form assistance at legalaid.wa.gov.au

### Eligibility — Grants of Aid

Legal Aid WA applies three tests before granting funded representation:

1. **Means test**: Income and assets below the threshold
2. **Merit test**: Case has reasonable prospects of success (not applied for criminal matters where liberty is at risk)
3. **Guidelines test**: Matter falls within Legal Aid WA's priority areas

**Priority areas**: Criminal (particularly custody matters), family law, domestic violence/restraining orders, mental health

**Apply**: Online at legalaid.wa.gov.au/apply or phone 1300 650 579

### Family Advocacy Support Service (FASS)

Specialist support for respondents in FVRO proceedings:
- Available at FVRO conferences (shuttle format)
- Provides on-the-day advice and support for respondents
- **To arrange**: Call 1300 650 579 before your conference date
- Available at major metropolitan Magistrates Courts

## 5. Duty Lawyer Services (by Court)

Duty lawyers provide free, on-the-day legal assistance. No appointment needed — attend on the day your matter is listed.

| Court | Duty Lawyer Provider | Notes |
|-------|---------------------|-------|
| **Magistrates Courts (all major)** | Legal Aid WA | Criminal and civil matters; FVRO respondents can request FASS |
| **District Court** | Legal Aid WA | Criminal and civil; arrange through court registry |
| **Supreme Court** | Legal Aid WA | Limited; criminal matters priority |
| **Family Court of WA** | Legal Aid WA | Family law duty lawyer — attend family law registry |
| **Children's Court** | Legal Aid WA | Youth justice and child protection |
| **Mental Health Tribunal** | MHAS (Mental Health Advocacy Service) | 1800 999 057 — contact MHAS before your Tribunal hearing |
| **FVRO Conferences** | Legal Aid WA — FASS | Call 1300 650 579 before the conference date to arrange |

### How Duty Lawyers Work

1. Arrive at court early (ideally 30–45 minutes before your listed time)
2. Identify yourself to the duty lawyer desk or registry
3. The duty lawyer will speak with you briefly and advise on your options
4. They may appear for you on the day for short procedural matters
5. For more complex representation, they can assist you to apply for a grant of aid
