## 1. Current FVRO Forms

WA Magistrates Court uses **descriptive titles** rather than sequential form numbers for most FVRO documents. Key forms:

### Application for FVRO

| Document | Title | Who Uses | Format |
|----------|-------|----------|--------|
| FVRO Application | *Application — Family Violence Restraining Order or Violence Restraining Order* | Applicant (protected person or police) | PDF or DOC available from magistratescourt.wa.gov.au |
| Supporting Affidavit | *Affidavit in Support of Application for FVRO/VRO* | Applicant | Required with application |

> **Verification note**: Filing channels for initial FVRO applications and legal-service-provider online applications can change. Check the Magistrates Court WA restraining orders page, the eCourts Portal, or the registry before telling a user a new application must be filed only one way.

---

### Respondent Documents

| Document | Title / Form | Section | Who Uses | Notes |
|----------|-------------|---------|----------|-------|
| Objection to interim FVRO | Respondent's endorsed copy of the interim order (returned to registrar) | s.31 | Respondent | Complete the endorsement section of the served copy and return within 21 days; no separate form typically required |
| Application to Vary or Cancel | **Form 12** — *Restraining Order — Application to Vary or Cancel* | s.45 | Respondent or other eligible parties | Available from magistratescourt.wa.gov.au; lodge with supporting affidavit |
| Supporting Affidavit | Affidavit in Support (respondent drafts this) | s.45–46 | Respondent | See `affidavit-templates.md` for templates |

## 2. How to File — In Person vs eLodgment

### In-Person Filing

**Commonly required for**:
- Initial FVRO applications by individuals, unless the current court guidance provides an online pathway
- First filing in a matter where no eCourts file exists
- Affidavits and applications in matters without an eCourts Portal file number

**How**:
1. Attend any Magistrates Court registry during business hours
2. Bring: completed form + supporting affidavit + all annexures (copies for court, for each party, and your own copy)
3. Registry staff will stamp and file; you receive a file number
4. Court fee payable at counter (see Filing Fees below)

**Tip**: Arrive early — registry queues at major courts (Perth, Joondalup) can be long.

---

### eLodgment via eCourts Portal

**Available for**:
- Documents in **existing matters** that already have an eCourts Portal file reference
- Follow-up applications (e.g., Form 12 variation/cancellation on an existing FVRO file)
- Supporting affidavits for ongoing matters
- New initial FVRO applications only if current court guidance provides that pathway

**How**:
1. Go to ecourts.justice.wa.gov.au
2. Register for or log into your eCourts Portal account
3. Navigate to *eLodgment* → locate the matter by file number
4. Upload documents (PDF format required)
5. Pay any filing fee by credit/debit card
6. You will receive a lodgment confirmation email

## 3. Respondent Procedure Step-by-Step

### Stage 1: Served with Interim FVRO

1. **Read the order carefully**: Identify every condition — no-contact, exclusion zones, surrender of weapons, any other restriction
2. **Note the service date**: Your 21-day objection period runs from the date you were served
3. **Comply immediately**: ALL conditions are binding from the moment of service, regardless of any planned objection
4. **Obtain legal advice immediately**: Contact Legal Aid WA duty lawyer or a private lawyer
5. **Do NOT contact the protected person** — even to discuss the order, even if invited

### Stage 2: Deciding Whether to Object (s.31)

If you dispute the allegations or seek different conditions:
1. Complete the **respondent's endorsement section** on the served copy of the interim order
2. Return to the **registrar of the court that made the order** within **21 days of service**
3. The registrar will arrange a conference (shuttle format, parties in separate rooms) or schedule a hearing
4. The interim order **remains in force** throughout — keep complying

If you do NOT object within 21 days: The court may make a final FVRO without a hearing.

### Stage 3: Conference

Most objections go first to a **shuttle conference**:
- Registrar moves between parties (you never meet the protected person)
- Goal: explore whether a consensual resolution is possible (e.g., agreed conditions, undertakings)
- Legal Aid WA's **Family Advocacy Support Service (FASS)** provides support to respondents at conference — call 1300 650 579 in advance to arrange
- If agreement is reached: consent order may be made
- If no agreement: matter proceeds to final hearing

### Stage 4: Final Hearing

Both parties attend. Affidavit evidence is filed. Witnesses may give evidence and be cross-examined. Court decides whether to:
- Make a final FVRO (same or varied conditions)
- Dismiss the application

### Stage 5: Variation or Cancellation (s.45 Application)

After final FVRO is made, or if seeking to vary an interim:
1. Complete **Form 12** — Application to Vary or Cancel
2. Draft supporting affidavit (see `affidavit-templates.md`)
3. File Form 12 + affidavit + annexures at registry or via eCourts Portal (if existing file)
4. Court schedules **ex parte leave hearing** (s.46) — protected person not present
5. If leave granted → substantive hearing (both parties)
6. If leave refused → application dismissed

## 4. Service Requirements

### Service on Respondent (of Interim FVRO)

Interim FVROs are generally served by:
- **Police**: Most common — police serve the order and note the date and method on the order
- **Court bailiff**: Available in some circumstances
- **Registered post**: Only where court has made an order for substituted service

The service date is critical — it starts the 21-day objection clock.

### Service of Respondent's Documents on Protected Person

For a s.45 variation/cancellation application, the court will generally serve the protected person after leave is granted. Before leave, the hearing is ex parte and service on the protected person is not required.

For other documents (e.g., in final hearing), personal service or substituted service may be required. Consult the court registry or a lawyer.

## 6. Filing Fees

| Matter | Fee (check current fee schedule) |
|--------|----------------------------------|
| FVRO application (protected person) | **No fee** — free to apply |
| FVRO variation/cancellation application | **No fee** — free to apply |
| Form 12 filing | Check current fee schedule at magistratescourt.wa.gov.au |
| Civil claim filing (Magistrates Court) | $97–$495 depending on amount claimed |
| Criminal matters (defendant) | Generally no filing fee |

**Fee waiver**: If a fee applies, hardship waiver/remission options may be available. Check the current court fee waiver process and required evidence.

> Always check the current fee schedule at magistratescourt.wa.gov.au or courts.justice.wa.gov.au as fees change periodically.

## 7. eCourts Portal Guide

**URL**: ecourts.justice.wa.gov.au

**What you can do**:
- View your existing matter file (if you have a matter number)
- Lodge documents in existing FVRO matters (eLodgment)
- Pay fees online
- View court dates and listings

**What may require registry filing or current guidance check**:
- A new initial FVRO application by an individual applicant
- Access matters where you are not a party

**Creating an account**:
1. Go to ecourts.justice.wa.gov.au
2. Click *Register* → complete account creation
3. Once registered, link to your existing matter using the file/case number from court documents

**Supported document formats**: PDF (preferred); some courts accept DOC — check specific court guidance.

**Technical help**: eCourts help line — (08) 9425 2222 or see magistratescourt.wa.gov.au

## Checklist: Filing a Form 12 (Variation/Cancellation)

- [ ] Completed Form 12 (download from magistratescourt.wa.gov.au)
- [ ] Supporting affidavit in correct WA Magistrates Court format (numbered paragraphs, jurat)
- [ ] All annexures prepared (signed by deponent and JP/lawyer; labelled Annexure A, B, C...)
- [ ] Copies made: one for court file + one for protected person (if served) + one for your records
- [ ] Filing fee (if applicable) — check current fee schedule; fee waiver form if needed
- [ ] Affidavit signed and sworn/affirmed before JP, lawyer, or court registrar before filing
- [ ] Lodge: in person at registry or via eCourts Portal eLodgment (if existing eCourts file)
